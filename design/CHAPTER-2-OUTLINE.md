# Chapter 2 — The Waiting
## Scene-by-Scene Outline · DRAFT v0.1

**Tone:** Slow dread under a normal surface. The chapter looks like routine guild work. It feels like holding your breath.
**Format:** Backdrop + portrait dialogue. Short boxes for banter, longer passages for the tension beats.
**Hale:** Minimal speaker. One line in this chapter. It's the most important one.

### Cast

- **Hale, Cinnia, Tobin, Hearthgar** — The core four from Chapter 1.
- **Bessa** — NPC. The guild's logistics backbone. Handles contracts, supplies, payroll. Not a fighter, not a commander. Aldric left her in charge because she keeps things running. Her "wait" is never cruel; it's the same pragmatism that keeps the guild solvent and its people fed. She is not the antagonist. The silence from the frontier is the antagonist.
- **Milla** — NPC-turned-recruit. An established courier in Vessia's capital, not a guild member. The party knows her as the fastest runner on the frontier routes. She's their link to outside news. Throughout the chapter they go to her for word from the east. She joins the party when they set out for Greywick, because her roads are dying and she's done pretending that's someone else's problem.
- **Marlowe & Brant** — Introduced mid-chapter through a board quest. A merchant's cargo was stolen, leading the party to a coastal wreck site where they find a pirate fencer and his anchor-wielding first mate fighting proto-Hollowed on the beach. Former pirates turned reluctant good guys. They join the guild after the rescue and volunteer for the Greywick run.
- **Aldric** — Absent. His absence IS the chapter.
- **Celia** — Background appearance only (not recruited until Ch. 4). Singing in a tavern.

### Narrative Arc

Chapter 2 has one emotional through-line: **the growing distance between "everything is fine" and the truth.** Each mission widens the gap. The board quests are normal. The guild functions. But Aldric isn't back, and every day that passes makes "he's fine" harder to believe. The chapter ends when the party stops believing it.

## Mission 2-1 — The Departure
**Teaching:** None (story mission)
**Location:** Guild Hall, then the eastern gate

### No battle. Story only.

Morning at the guild. Aldric is gearing up. He's assembled a team: Garrick and several veteran guild members (NPCs). The party watches the preparations.

Aldric pulls the core four aside before he leaves.

> **Aldric:** "I need you here. That's not a punishment. The guild doesn't stop because I'm on the road, and somebody has to keep Cinnia from feeding the whole capital."
> **Cinnia:** "I would NEVER—"
> **Aldric:** "You absolutely would, and I love you for it."

He introduces Bessa.

> **Aldric:** "Bessa runs this place better than I do. I just swing a sword. She makes sure everyone gets paid and nobody starves."
> **Bessa:** "He's exaggerating. Slightly."

Bessa is calm, practical, and clearly fond of Aldric. She's not in competition with him. They're two halves of the same operation.

Aldric kneels down to Tobin's level.

> **Aldric:** "Keep reading the water for me, little tide. If it tells you anything interesting, write it down. I want to hear everything when I get back."
> **Tobin:** "...Promise you'll come back?"
> **Aldric:** "Always do."

He ruffles Tobin's hair. Claps Hearthgar's shoulder (the suit clangs). Nods to Hale. Then to Cinnia:

> **Aldric:** "Save me a bowl?"
> **Cinnia:** "Always."

He walks to the gate with his team. The party watches them until the road bends out of sight.

The backdrop holds on the empty road for one beat too long before the scene transitions.

## Mission 2-2 — Business as Usual
**Teaching:** Repeatable board quest depth, reward structure
**Location:** Roads near the capital

### Pre-battle

Next day. Bessa has the board running. She's efficient and organized, assigning contracts with the briskness of someone who's done this for years.

> **Bessa:** "Supply escort to the northern farms. Straightforward. The merchant specifically requested 'the ones with the big armor and the floating water,' so I assume that's you."

Light banter. The mood is still okay. Aldric just left yesterday; no one's worried yet.

Hearthgar is trying too hard to seem relaxed.

> **Hearthgar:** "This is fine. Normal day. Normal quest."
> **Cinnia:** "You've said 'normal' three times."
> **Hearthgar:** "...Normal amount of times."

### Battle

Routine board quest. Escort fight against standard fauna. Nothing Hollowed. The world still feels safe near the capital.

### Post-battle

> **Tobin:** "The water says the merchant's cart has a loose axle."
> **Tobin:** "Also that it'll rain tomorrow."
> **Cinnia:** "Tobin."
> **Tobin:** "And it's always—"
> **Cinnia:** "—right, yes."

Running gag, comfortable version. Everything is still okay.

## Mission 2-3 — The Ballad of the Unbroken
**Teaching:** None (atmosphere/foreshadowing)
**Location:** Wayfarer's Inn

### No battle. Story and atmosphere.

Evening. The party is at the inn after a day's work. A traveling songstress is performing. This is **Celia** — not named yet, not recruited, just a woman with a lyre and a voice that fills the room.

She's singing a ballad about Aldric the Unbroken.

The song is described, not transcribed in full. A few key lines come through in the dialogue boxes:

> *"...who held the bridge at Cairnmoor alone / who carried the fallen on his shield..."*

The room listens. It's clearly a well-known song. Soldiers hum along. The bartender mouths the words.

Character reactions during the ballad:

> **Cinnia:** (quietly, smiling) "He hates this song, you know. Says it makes him sound taller than he is."
> **Hearthgar:** "He IS tall."
> **Tobin:** "The water gets warm when she sings. That's never happened before."

The songstress finishes. The inn applauds. She takes a small bow and starts tuning for the next one.

This scene exists for one reason: so that when this exact melody returns in Ch. 7 as a slow, broken lament over the party fighting Aldric's Hollowed reflection, the player remembers the inn. The warm room. Cinnia smiling. The water getting warm.

**Design note:** If the game has any kind of audio or music-cue system, this is where the "Aldric's Ballad" leitmotif is introduced.

## Mission 2-4 — Wreck of the Cerulean Wake
**Teaching:** New unit integration, rescue encounters
**Location:** Coastal cliffs south of the capital

### Pre-battle

Day four. A board quest: a merchant reports stolen cargo from a coastal supply route. Routine recovery job. Bessa assigns it.

> **Bessa:** "Cargo theft on the south coast. Probably bandits. Bring the crates back; I don't care about the thieves."

The party follows the trail to the coast and finds something worse than bandits. A ship is smashed on the rocks, hull split open, cargo scattered across the beach. Proto-Hollowed creatures are swarming the wreckage.

And in the middle of it, two men are fighting back-to-back.

**Marlowe** — rapier out, lace cuffs ruined, fighting with theatrical precision even when nobody's watching. **Brant** — swinging a ship's anchor on a chain, silent, immovable, keeping Marlowe alive through sheer mass.

> **Marlowe:** (mid-parry, spotting the party) "Oh WONDERFUL. An audience. Brant, we have an audience!"
> **Brant:** "Fighting."
> **Marlowe:** "Yes, obviously, but with STYLE now."

### Battle

Rescue encounter. The party joins Marlowe and Brant against waves of proto-Hollowed creatures on the beach. Marlowe and Brant are temporary allies during the fight, showcasing their combat styles: Marlowe's Flourish passive (more damage when he acts after allies) and Brant's massive single-hit break damage.

The creatures are coming from the water. Something drove them up from the coast. The wreck isn't the cause; the ship was fleeing something and didn't make it.

### Post-battle

The beach goes quiet. Marlowe sheathes his rapier with a flourish, then immediately sits down on a crate because his legs are shaking. He's been fighting for hours.

> **Marlowe:** "Marlowe, formerly captain of the Cerulean Wake. Formerly, as in... well." (He gestures at the wreck.) "The sea went wrong two days ago. Water stopped moving in patches. Then those things came up from the shallows."
> **Brant:** "Anchor held."
> **Marlowe:** "The anchor held, yes. The SHIP did not."

The party helps recover the merchant's cargo from the wreckage. Marlowe and Brant have nowhere to go. Their ship is driftwood.

> **Cinnia:** "Come back to the guild. There's food and a roof."
> **Marlowe:** "Madam, I was a pirate three weeks ago. I'm not sure your guild—"
> **Cinnia:** "There's food. And a roof."

Beat.

> **Marlowe:** "...Brant?"
> **Brant:** "Hungry."

They come back. Bessa processes them with the same brisk efficiency she applies to everything.

> **Bessa:** "Pirates?"
> **Marlowe:** "Formerly."
> **Bessa:** "Can you fight?"
> **Brant:** "Anchor."
> **Bessa:** "...Sign here."

Marlowe and Brant join the roster. The stolen cargo is returned.

**Underneath the comedy:** The sea going wrong. Proto-Hollowed coming from the water. The Hollowing isn't just spreading overland. And this is the fourth day with no word from Aldric. Nobody mentions it. That's the point.

## Mission 2-5 — Any Word?
**Teaching:** None (story / tension)
**Location:** Milla's courier post in Castle Town

### No battle. Story only.

Day six. Still no word from Aldric. The party goes looking for answers.

**Milla** runs a courier post near the market district. She's not a guild member. She's a freelance courier, the fastest in Vessia, and everyone knows it. Winged boots, overstuffed satchel, a cloak that crackles with static even indoors. She doesn't stop moving, even behind a counter.

The party finds her sorting parcels. Cinnia asks the question directly.

> **Cinnia:** "Milla. Have you heard anything from the eastern frontier? Any runners, any deliveries, anything?"
> **Milla:** "From the east? Not in four days. That's not normal. I always get something from the east."

She pulls out a route ledger. Points to gaps in her delivery log.

> **Milla:** "Two of my regular frontier routes are dead. Not blocked, not washed out. Just... nothing moves on them anymore. The birds stopped. I dropped a parcel at a crossroads last week and it was still there when I came back. Nobody picked it up. Nobody walked past it."
> **Milla:** "I ran faster than usual that day."

> **Tobin:** "We need to know if a guild team came through. Headed east. Ten days ago."
> **Milla:** "Big group? Veteran types? Yeah, I saw them pass through the eastern gate. They didn't use my service. Had their own runners."
> **Milla:** "...Those runners haven't come back either, have they."

She says it like she already knows the answer.

> **Cinnia:** "No."

Milla closes the ledger. The party leaves. No one says anything on the walk back.

**Back at the guild:**

> **Cinnia:** "Has anyone heard from Aldric's team?"
> **Bessa:** (a pause, barely perceptible) "Not yet. That's normal for frontier operations. Long roads, difficult terrain."
> **Cinnia:** "It's been six days, Bessa."
> **Bessa:** "I know how many days it's been."

She says it quietly. Not dismissive. She's counting too.

## Mission 2-6 — Closer
**Teaching:** Escalation / difficulty check
**Location:** Supply road, closer to the capital than 1-7

### Pre-battle

Day seven. A board quest: clear a supply road that's had reports of aggressive wildlife. Routine. Except:

> **Tobin:** "...It's like 1-7 again."
> **Cinnia:** "Don't say that."
> **Tobin:** "The water's showing me the same blankness. Closer to the capital this time."

They find proto-Hollowed creatures on a road that was safe a week ago. Whatever is happening on the frontier is spreading toward them.

### Battle

Harder fight than anything in Chapter 2 so far. Multiple proto-Hollowed enemies, mixed elements. The creatures are more aggressive and more visibly wrong than the ones in Ch. 1. Some are partially crystalline now, not just translucent.

### Post-battle

The party stands among the glassy remains. The creatures shattered like the ones in 1-7, but louder. More of them.

> **Hearthgar:** "These were closer. A lot closer."
> **Cinnia:** "...It's been eight days. Since Aldric left."

Nobody says what they're thinking. Cinnia starts packing the field kit. Her hands are busy. Tobin's water orbs are perfectly still, which is worse than when they're agitated.

**Back at the guild**, Bessa is reviewing the report. She reads it twice.

> **Bessa:** "I'll increase the patrol radius. And I'll put out a runner request for any news from the eastern frontier."
> **Cinnia:** "Bessa. It's been a week."
> **Bessa:** "I know how long it's been."

She says it quietly. Not dismissive. She's counting the days too.

## Mission 2-7 — Milla's Run
**Teaching:** Escort/terrain difficulty
**Location:** Eastern road, toward the frontier

### Pre-battle

Day nine. Still nothing. The party goes back to Milla's courier post. They ask her to scout the eastern road. Not as a guild job. As a favor.

> **Cinnia:** "We need someone who knows those roads. Really knows them. Can you get close enough to see what's happening out there?"
> **Milla:** "Alone? I'm fast, not stupid."
> **Cinnia:** "We'll go with you."

The party convinces Bessa to authorize a limited-range scouting run. Go as far as the crossroads east of the capital. If the road is passable, Milla pushes ahead. If not, they come back.

> **Bessa:** "The crossroads. Not a step further. I mean it."

Milla joins as a temporary party member for this mission.

### Battle

The road is bad. Proto-Hollowed creatures in increasing density. The party fights through multiple encounters on what should be a half-day journey. The environment itself is wrong: trees with bark that's going translucent, a stream that doesn't make sound.

### Post-battle

They reach the crossroads. The road to Greywick stretches east. From here, they can see the horizon. Something is wrong with the sky in that direction. Not dark, not cloudy. Just... flat. Like a painting where the artist forgot the depth.

Milla scouts ahead on foot. She comes back running.

> **Milla:** "The road's open, but barely. I got a mile past the crossroads. There's smoke east of here. Old smoke, not fresh. And the silence is... I've never heard nothing that loud."
> **Milla:** "I can't tell if your team went through there or around. But someone went through. There are tracks."

The party returns to report. Bessa listens.

> **Bessa:** "Thank you. I'll file this with the garrison and request a senior patrol."
> **Cinnia:** "A senior patrol. That could take days."
> **Bessa:** "Yes. It could."

She's not wrong. Senior patrols require authorization from the garrison commander. Requisition forms. Scheduling. The bureaucracy of safety.

The party leaves her office. The hallway is quiet.

> **Cinnia:** "I can't do this for days more."
> **Tobin:** "The water's been showing me the same thing all week. Blank. Blank. Blank. And then... just for a second... it showed me Aldric's face."
> **Hearthgar:** "Was he—"
> **Tobin:** "I don't know. The water went blank again."

## Mission 2-8 — The Gate
**Teaching:** None (story climax of the chapter)
**Location:** Guild Hall, then the eastern gate

### No battle. Story only.

Day ten. Morning. The party gathers in the mess hall. Cinnia hasn't cooked. That's how the player knows.

> **Marlowe:** "No breakfast? That's... unprecedented."
> **Cinnia:** "I'm packing trail rations."

She's been up all night. Three days' worth of food, packed in travel bundles. Enough for the whole party.

Hale is standing by the door. He hasn't said anything. He doesn't need to.

> **Hearthgar:** "We're going. Aren't we."
> (Not a question.)
> **Tobin:** "I packed my things last night."

The party walks to Bessa's office. Cinnia speaks for the group.

> **Cinnia:** "Bessa. It's been ten days. Aldric said it was a three-day operation. We're going."
> **Bessa:** "I can't authorize that."
> **Cinnia:** "I know."
> **Bessa:** "You're junior members. The frontier is deteriorating. I have no intel on what happened to the advance team. Sending you out there is—"
> **Cinnia:** "I know."

Beat.

> **Bessa:** "...I can't authorize it. I can't stop you, either. You're not soldiers. This isn't the army. But I want you to understand that I'm right, and you know I'm right, and you're going anyway."
> **Cinnia:** "Yes."
> **Bessa:** "Then at least take the route Milla scouted."

She pulls out a map. Marks the crossroads, the path Milla found, the last known direction of the tracks.

Bessa isn't angry. She's scared for them, and too professional to show it, and too honest to hide it completely.

The party moves to the gate. Marlowe and Brant are already there, packs on.

> **Marlowe:** "For the record, nobody told US to stay. Technically, we haven't received any orders at all."
> **Brant:** "Volunteering."
> **Marlowe:** "What he said."

Milla is leaning against the gate post, travel pack already on. She's not a guild member. Nobody asked her to be here.

> **Cinnia:** "Milla? What are you—"
> **Milla:** "My routes are dead. Every road east of here has gone quiet. That's not 'someone else's problem' anymore. That's my life."
> **Milla:** "You need someone who knows those roads. I need to know why they stopped talking."

The full party assembles. Seven people at the gate.

**Hale's one line in Chapter 2.** He's been silent. He walks to the front of the group. Turns to face them. Doesn't give a speech. Doesn't rally anyone. Just:

> **Hale:** "Let's go."

Two words. The party moves.

As they pass through the gate, there's a supply kit sitting on the stone ledge. Packed tight. A note on top in Bessa's handwriting:

> *"Eat the rations. Don't be stupid. Come back."*

Cinnia picks it up. She doesn't say anything. She doesn't have to.

The backdrop shows the party on the eastern road, walking toward the flat horizon. The guild hall shrinks behind them.

**Chapter 2 ends.** The waiting is over. Whatever is at Greywick, they're walking into it with their eyes open and their orders unsigned.

## Pacing Note

This chapter has four combat missions (2-2 routine, 2-4 shipwreck, 2-6 escalation, 2-7 scouting run) compared to Ch. 1's five. That's deliberate. The tension lives in the waiting, not the fighting. Combat escalates steadily: routine board quest, then a coastal wreck with proto-Hollowed, then urban escalation, then a road that shouldn't be this dangerous. But the story scenes carry the chapter's weight. The player should feel restless by 2-7, not because the game is boring, but because the characters are restless and it's contagious.
