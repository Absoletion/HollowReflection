# Hale — 4★ Canonical Model Specification

**Status:** Canon lock for future corrections and generations  
**Visual classification:** Dark with Hollow influence  
**Gameplay element:** Retains the existing Hollow gameplay tag until an explicit systems decision changes it  
**Form:** Forced story evolution at Chapter Six

## Authority order

When two generated images disagree, use this order:

1. **`Hale 4 star Character Turnaround.png`** — costume construction, body proportions, front/back relationships, cape, and physical sword.
2. **Original `Hale-4star-reference.png`** — rendering ambition, material richness, heroic energy, and key-art spectacle.
3. **`Hale VFX sheet.png`** — effect geometry and the Dark/Hollow relationship.
4. **`Hale Face and Expressions.png`** — expression vocabulary, normalized to the facial model below.
5. **`Hale Arts Cut In.png`** — cut-in staging and text-safe composition only.
6. **`Hale Battle Rig Source Design.png`** — simplification target only; not a layer or costume authority.

No generated asset may introduce a permanent costume or weapon change merely because it appears in one image.

## Identity lock

- Young adult male; composed, burdened, and capable rather than cruel or feral.
- Lean athletic build with heroic anime proportions.
- Tousled moon-silver hair with cool gray-violet shadows.
- Narrow red eyes with dark upper lashes.
- Tapered but not overly delicate jaw.
- Straight compact nose and restrained mouth.
- Red branching scar on **Hale's left side of the neck**.
- High black armored collar with plum inner lining.
- Dark-primary silhouette framed by polished silver planes.
- No horns, natural wings, claws, fangs, or demonic anatomy.

## Hair and face construction

Use the turnaround's overall head size and the expression sheet's front-facing clarity.

- Hair is built from a broad swept fringe, layered side locks, a crown break, and outward rear spikes.
- The fringe crosses the forehead asymmetrically without covering both eyes.
- Preserve a few larger locks rather than multiplying fine strands.
- Red eyes remain a story/identity accent, not a reflection of current VFX color.
- Neutral Hale looks observant and guarded. Confidence appears through posture and eye focus, not a constant smirk.
- Facial injuries are temporary overlays; the neck scar is permanent.

## Canonical armor construction

Directions are named from Hale's perspective.

- **Left shoulder:** blackened, ornate, layered pauldron with sharper gothic edging.
- **Right shoulder:** brighter polished-silver pauldron with simpler construction.
- **Chest:** asymmetrically divided blackened and polished plate, following the turnaround's diagonal rhythm.
- **Undersuit:** fitted charcoal-black fabric with visible mobility at shoulders, elbows, hips, and knees.
- **Harness:** warm dark-brown leather crossing the torso and securing the cape/weapon system.
- **Forearms:** articulated steel bracers over dark gloves; left/right detail may differ but attachment logic remains consistent.
- **Waist:** broad leather belt and secondary straps with a single faceted arsenal crest at center.
- **Hips:** polished side plates over dark split coat panels.
- **Legs:** dark fitted trousers, articulated silver knee/shin protection, and armored black boots.
- **Cape:** deep plum-black, attached at the upper back/shoulders, long and split for movement.

Engraving is concentrated at the left pauldron, selected chest edges, bracers, belt crest, sword guard, and cape emblem. Do not cover every plate in equally dense filigree.

## Cape emblem

The canonical cape emblem is a vertically symmetrical silver gothic mark combining:

- A central spear or blade axis.
- Four outward cathedral-flame or fleur-like lobes.
- A pointed lower termination.

It is not a skull, animal, royal crest, or religious cross unless later lore explicitly defines it. Create one clean vector master and reuse it without generative reinterpretation.

## Canonical physical sword

Hale carries **one physical sword**. Floating blade-stars are conjured arsenal effects, not extra copies of the weapon.

- Oversized straight double-edged blade.
- Broad silver bevels and bright point.
- Black-violet central Hollowglass channel.
- One elongated diamond-shaped internal fracture motif.
- Ornate dark cathedral/bat-wing guard with silver edge planes.
- Purple-black wrapped grip.
- Pointed spearhead-like pommel.
- The side view must retain credible blade thickness; it cannot collapse into a needle.

The sword in the turnaround is the construction authority. The original key art governs its desired material richness and energy response.

## Dark/Hollow visual ratio

### Dark — approximately 80%

- Violet-black crescents, thorns, shadow mass, and forceful sweeping motion.
- Saturated arc-violet emission with small magenta notes.
- Predatory inward curves and sharp trailing edges.
- Active, dangerous, and visibly energetic.

### Hollow — approximately 20%

- Pale glass-teal or nearly colorless refraction.
- Transparent shards and internal cracks.
- Incomplete cathedral circles and missing geometry.
- Suspended fragments that feel briefly silent amid the Dark motion.

Dark forms the attack. Hollow appears inside it, interrupts it, or reveals that the power is not entirely elemental.

## Canonical VFX set

- **Skill trail:** violet-black crescent with a narrow hot core and sparse glass-teal debris.
- **Art activation:** incomplete circular gothic ring, fractured at deliberate points.
- **Burst sigil:** large cathedral-window geometry with pale Hollow interior and Dark outer frame.
- **Blade-star:** four-point dark projectile with a pale refractive core.
- **Ground impact:** dark spikes surrounding a concentrated silver-white strike point and outward glass fragments.
- **Shard field:** suspended translucent pieces connected by sparse dark curves or tension lines.
- **Aura:** low, thorned violet-black shadow flame with limited bloom.
- **Hollow fracture:** pale interior tear visible through a Dark outer shell.

The VFX sheet is a concept authority only. Every runtime effect must be recreated separately on real transparency without labels.

## Rarity continuity

### 3★ retained identifiers

- Same face, hair, red eyes, and left-neck scar.
- High collar, practical black clothing, warm leather, partial steel plate.
- One simpler shoulder assembly.
- Shorter split coat/cape silhouette.
- Same sword family with a narrower Hollowglass channel.
- Isolated shards and incomplete arc fragments only.

The existing 3★ generated art overstates the completed halo. Corrected 3★ art should not display the full cathedral circle or a 4★ density of blade-stars.

### 4★ transformation

- Completes the asymmetric armor construction.
- Lengthens and formalizes the cape.
- Fully awakens the black-violet Hollowglass sword channel.
- Introduces the complete cape emblem.
- Unlocks deliberate cathedral-ring and blade-star control.

## Approval checklist

Before approving any new Hale image:

- Does the face match the canonical jaw, eyes, hair mass, and apparent age?
- Is the scar on Hale's left neck?
- Are the left and right pauldrons correctly differentiated?
- Does the chest plate follow the same asymmetric division?
- Is there exactly one physical sword?
- Does the sword match the canonical guard, channel, profile, and pommel?
- Is the cape emblem unchanged?
- Is Dark visually primary and Hollow secondary?
- Are effects separable from the body where production requires it?
- Is any claimed transparency genuine rather than a baked checkerboard?
