# Chapter 1 — The Guild Charter
## Scene-by-Scene Outline · DRAFT v0.2

**Tone:** Deliberately warm and ordinary. This chapter is the home the player will miss.
**Format:** Mixed VN. Dialogue displayed over a backdrop with character portraits matched to the speaker. Short boxes (2-3 lines) for banter and combat. Longer passages for scene-setting and key moments.
**Hale:** Minimal speaker. Speaks only at marked points. Others carry conversation and react to his implied responses.

### Combat System (current)

- **Skill** — The basic action. Tap to use; generates Arts gauge (amount varies per character for balance). Each character's Skill is unique.
- **Arts** — Costs 100 Arts gauge. The role-defining move.
- **Burst** — Costs 200 Arts gauge. The ultimate.
- No guard mechanic. No separate basic attack. Skills ARE the basic loop.

### Cast

- **Hale** — Arrives seeking guild membership. Quiet, capable, no backstory given yet. His ordinariness is the point.
- **Cinnia** — The guild's cook, already a member. Cheerful, nurturing, absent-minded about everything except food and hurt people. Starter unit.
- **Tobin** — Fortune-teller kid, already a member. Aldric vouched for him when the guild said he was too young. Earnest, reads water. Starter unit.
- **Hearthgar** — Nervous teen bonded to a derelict ember-golem suit. Aldric taught him that being scared and standing anyway is the whole job. Already a member.
- **Aldric the Unbroken** — NPC. The guild's living legend. Openly warm, laughs easily, teases the recruits like family. More Iroh than Auron. The guild loves him because he's genuinely lovable, not just respected. He remembers everyone's name, their habits, their fears. He runs Hale's entrance exam personally not because it's protocol, but because he wants to meet everyone who walks through the door. Five roster backstories hook into him. He will become Hollowed Boss 2 in Ch. 7-8, so every laugh, every nickname, every kind word here is load-bearing.

### Narrative Arc Within Chapter 1

The chapter moves through three emotional beats: **arrival** (1-1 through 1-3), **belonging** (1-4 through 1-6), and **purpose** (1-7 through 1-10). By the end, the guild feels like home and the frontier feels like it matters.

## Mission 1-1 — Guild Entrance Exam
**Teaching:** Skill activation
**Location:** Guild training yard

### Pre-battle

The game opens on Vessia's capital. Short establishing narration: a frontier kingdom, prosperous but restless. Reports from the borderlands have been strange lately. The Adventurers' Guild is hiring.

Hale arrives at the guild hall. The door is propped open with a cook pot (Cinnia's). Inside, the hall is busy but not imposing. Boards on the walls, people eating, someone arguing about a bounty.

**Aldric** is at the far end, laughing with two guild members over something. He's big, weathered, and immediately approachable. He spots Hale and waves him over before Hale has to ask.

> **Aldric:** "There he is. You're the one who signed up this morning, right? I read your form. Come on, let's see what you've got."
> (He claps Hale on the shoulder. Casual. Like they've already met.)
> **Aldric:** "Fair warning: the training golem hits back. Don't worry, I built it myself. It pulls its punches. ...Usually."

He leads Hale to the training yard, where a **Construct** (training golem) waits. Aldric explains Skills: each fighter has a core combat technique, their bread and butter.

> **Aldric:** "Skills are your foundation. Everything else you'll learn builds on this. So show me yours, and don't hold back. The Construct can take it."

**Tutorial prompt:** Skill activation is taught here. The Construct is the opponent.

### Battle

Solo Hale vs. one Construct. The fight teaches Skill use (Cross Slash) and the Arts gauge (Skills generate Arts). Simple, low-stakes. The Construct is slow and telegraphed.

### Post-battle

Aldric grins. Not surprised, but pleased.

> **Aldric:** "Ha! Clean hit. You've trained with someone good, or you've trained with nobody and you're a natural. Either way, I like it."
> **Aldric:** "Come on. You haven't eaten yet, and Cinnia will never forgive me if I let a new recruit go hungry."

He walks Hale toward the mess hall like it's the most important stop on the tour. Transition to:

**Cinnia's stew scene.** The game's first warm moment. She's already feeding Hearthgar and Tobin. The kitchen is too small for the golem suit, so Hearthgar is wedged in a corner with his bowl balanced on a gauntlet. Tobin's water orbs are orbiting his head while he eats.

> **Cinnia:** "Oh! New face! Sit, sit — I just pulled the pot off. You look half-starved."
> (She's already handing him a bowl before he responds.)
> **Tobin:** "He passed the exam. I saw it in the basin this morning."
> **Cinnia:** "Tobin, you say that about everyone."
> **Tobin:** "And I'm always right!"

Cinnia introduces herself. Tobin introduces himself (proudly). Hearthgar tries to introduce himself but bumps the table with the suit, rattling every bowl. He apologizes twice.

> **Hearthgar:** "S-sorry — the suit doesn't — it's fine, everything's fine."

Cinnia doesn't flinch. She refills his bowl.

This is the foundational character scene. The player meets three party members and the mentor in the first five minutes, and the vibe is family dinner, not military briefing.

**Hale's line (his first):**
> **Hale:** "...Good stew."

Two words. Cinnia beams. That's the scene.

## Mission 1-2 — Second Construct
**Teaching:** Arts Energy and Arts activation
**Location:** Guild training yard

### Pre-battle

Next morning. Aldric has Hale back in the yard with a tougher Construct. Cinnia tags along ("I should practice too") and is now a party member.

Aldric explains the Arts system: Skills build your Arts gauge. At 100, you can use an Art, your role-defining move.

> **Aldric:** "Every Skill you land fills the gauge a little. When it hits a hundred, you've got something real to spend. Cinnia, show him yours."
> **Cinnia:** "Oh! Right. Mine's more of a... battlefield lunch break."
> **Aldric:** (laughing) "It's saved more lives than half the swords in this guild."

**Tutorial prompt:** Arts gauge accumulation and Arts activation. Cinnia's Arts (Field Rations, party heal) is introduced here.

### Battle

Hale + Cinnia vs. a stronger Construct. The fight is designed so the player takes enough damage to need Cinnia's heal, which requires building to 100 Arts first. The loop clicks: Skill, build gauge, spend on Arts.

### Post-battle

> **Cinnia:** "See? Cooking IS combat-applicable."

Short scene. Tobin is watching from the fence, narrating the fight to his water orbs. Hearthgar was "standing guard at the gate" (hiding from the Construct).

## Mission 1-3 — Field Readiness
**Teaching:** Burst activation
**Location:** Guild training yard (final exam)

### Pre-battle

The third exam. Aldric brings the full training Construct — bigger, meaner. Tobin and Hearthgar are added to the party.

Aldric explains Bursts: at 200 Arts, you unlock your ultimate. The move that defines you.

> **Aldric:** "Two hundred. That's the big one. You'll feel it when you're close. Don't rush it, don't hoard it. Trust the moment."
> **Tobin:** "I've been practicing mine! The water gets really dramatic."
> **Aldric:** "It does. It's impressive, little tide."
> **Hearthgar:** "I... don't know if the suit's furnace can handle a full Burst yet."
> **Aldric:** "It can. I went over it last week. Tightened the intake valve, replaced a cracked seal. You're good."
> **Hearthgar:** "...You did that? When?"
> **Aldric:** "Tuesday. You were asleep. Don't worry about it."

He says it like it's nothing. Like maintaining a kid's magical furnace suit in the middle of the night is just what you do. After Ch. 7, this exchange is devastating.

**Tutorial prompt:** Burst activation. All four party members available. The Construct has enough HP to require a Burst to finish efficiently.

### Battle

Hale + Cinnia + Tobin + Hearthgar vs. the final training Construct. Full party combat tutorial.

### Post-battle

Aldric stamps Hale's guild charter. He holds it up, admires it for a second, and hands it over with both hands.

> **Aldric:** "Welcome to the guild, Hale. I mean that."
> (Beat.)
> **Aldric:** "Now let's eat. Cinnia's been stress-prepping since this morning and if we don't show up she'll feed it all to Tobin."

Cinnia insists on a celebration dinner. Aldric is the one who suggests a toast (with stew, because there's no ale in the mess hall). Hearthgar offers to help cook. Cinnia politely declines (the last time he helped, the furnace set the curtains on fire). Tobin's water put it out, and he hasn't let either of them forget. Aldric tells the curtain fire story better than anyone, complete with hand gestures.

**Transition:** The backdrop shows the guild hall at evening. Warm light, laughter, the feeling of a family dinner. This is the home.

## Mission 1-4 — Meet the Garrison
**Teaching:** Town and mission navigation
**Location:** Castle Town

### No battle. Exploration/dialogue mission.

Aldric gives Hale a personal tour of Castle Town. He knows everyone. The baker waves. A guard calls him by name. A kid runs up and he ruffles their hair without breaking stride.

- **Adventurer's Garrison (Castle):** Story missions launch from here. Aldric: "This is where the work starts. Not where it ends, mind you. The ending's always somewhere muddier."
- **Wayfarer's Inn:** Summoning, party management, unit library. The innkeeper greets Aldric by name and starts pouring a drink before he asks. Aldric: "When new faces show up looking to join, they end up here first. Keep an eye on this place."
- **Market:** Gold and future purchases. The merchant tries to overcharge Hale; Aldric gives the merchant a look and the price halves.
- **Training Yard:** Already familiar from the exams. Aldric points out a gouge in the training post. "That's Hearthgar's first day. He sneezed inside the suit."

Aldric pauses at the **Quest Board** outside the Inn. He reads a few postings, pulls one down that's outdated, pins a new one up himself.

> **Aldric:** "Not yet for you. Soon. Let's get you settled first."

The scene is functional (teaching the town UI) but peppered with character moments. Cinnia waves from the kitchen window and shouts something about leftovers. Tobin is reading fortunes for a merchant who doesn't believe him. Hearthgar accidentally blocks the market doorway and has to back out while apologizing to three people at once.

**Aldric's one quieter moment:** They pass a notice board with frontier reports. Aldric's eyes linger on one for a beat too long.

> **Aldric:** "Vessia's a good place, Hale. Strong people, good land. The frontier's been acting up lately, but that's what we're for."
> (He smiles, but the smile doesn't quite reach the pause before it.)
> **Aldric:** "Anyway. Have you tried the market's honey bread? Criminal how good it is."

He changes the subject. The player files away the pause.

## Mission 1-5 — The Quest Board
**Teaching:** Side missions / repeatable content
**Location:** Castle Town (Quest Board)

### Brief dialogue scene, then a short mission.

Aldric unlocks the Quest Board for Hale. Explains that contracts come from locals with problems.

> **Aldric:** "Not every job is glamorous. Some farmer's fence needs mending. Some merchant can't find a crate. But you know what? That farmer remembers you came. That merchant trusts the guild a little more. The small work is the real work."

The available quest is deliberately mundane: clear some pests from a supply road. Cinnia volunteers to come along, and Tobin tags himself in without being asked.

### Battle

Short, easy pest-clearing fight. Meant to show repeatable content.

### Post-battle

> **Tobin:** "The water says there'll be more of those tomorrow."
> **Cinnia:** "The water says a lot of things, Tobin."
> **Tobin:** "And it's always right!"

Running gag established.

## Mission 1-6 — First Contract
**Teaching:** Party formation
**Location:** A road outside the capital

### Pre-battle

The first real contract: something has been scaring caravans on the eastern road. Not Hollowed — just oversized fauna that wandered too close to the trade routes. Aldric assigns it to Hale and tells him to pick his squad.

**Tutorial prompt:** Party formation screen. The player arranges their 4 units for the first time. (Prior missions had fixed parties.)

Whoever Hale picks, Cinnia comments on it.

> **Cinnia (if picked):** "Obviously."
> **Cinnia (if not picked):** "...Fine. I'll make the victory dinner instead."

### Battle

Standard encounter — slightly harder than 1-5, multiple waves. The point is that the player chose their squad and feels ownership.

### Post-battle

The caravan merchant thanks them. Offers a tip. Cinnia refuses the tip and accepts a sack of flour instead.

> **Cinnia:** "Flour is worth more than coin in this kitchen."

## Mission 1-7 — Roadside Hollow
**Teaching:** Enemy elements
**Location:** A stretch of road near the frontier

### Pre-battle

A follow-up contract: something stranger on the road this time. The fauna isn't just big — it's acting wrong. A deer that won't run. A bird circling the same point for hours.

This is the chapter's first uneasy beat. The tone shifts subtly. The music gets quieter.

> **Tobin:** "...The water won't read this one."
> **Cinnia:** "What do you mean?"
> **Tobin:** "I mean it's showing me nothing. Like looking at a blank page."

They find a creature that's partially translucent. Not crystalline — not yet. Just wrong. Faintly luminous at the edges.

**Tutorial prompt:** Elemental wheel introduction. The creature has an element; the player learns about advantage and disadvantage.

### Battle

First encounter with a proto-Hollowed creature. It has an element (Earth or Thunder, depending on tuning). The fight teaches elemental matchups. The creature is disturbing but beatable.

### Post-battle

The creature shatters when it dies. Not like a normal enemy — it comes apart like glass, silently. The party stares.

> **Hearthgar:** "What... what WAS that?"
> **Cinnia:** "...I don't know."
> **Hale:** "..." (Silent. But his hand goes to his neck — the place where, later, the scar will be. An unconscious gesture the player won't notice on first playthrough.)

Nobody has an answer. They head back.

## Mission 1-8 — Return to the Guild
**Teaching:** Experience and levels
**Location:** Guild Hall

### No battle. Story and systems.

Back at the guild, they report to Aldric. He listens carefully, leaning forward, asking follow-up questions with the kind of focus that's different from his usual warmth.

> **Aldric:** "Translucent. At the edges, you said?"
> **Aldric:** "And when it went down, it shattered. Like glass."
> (He sits back. Rubs his jaw. Then the warmth comes back, a little too deliberately.)
> **Aldric:** "You did the right thing. All four of you. I'm proud of you."

He means it. But the questions were too specific, and the player might notice he didn't seem surprised. (On replay: he's already heard reports like this. The leaks are starting. And he just told four kids he's proud of them because he's scared for them.)

**Tutorial prompt:** Experience and leveling. Units have gained XP from the chapter's fights. The game teaches the player how to check levels and growth in the Training Yard.

Character scene: Cinnia is stress-cooking. She does this when she doesn't know what to do — cooks more food. The kitchen is overflowing. Tobin is eating his third bowl and slowing down. Hearthgar offers to take some to the garrison soldiers.

> **Cinnia:** "I just want to make sure everyone's fed. That's all."
> (She says it like she's convincing herself.)

Aldric reappears. He's lighter again, like he's made a decision and it's settled him.

> **Aldric:** "So. There's an old basin east of here. Been dry longer than I've been posted. Locals are saying the water came back, but it's... odd. Won't ripple right, they say."
> **Aldric:** "Probably nothing. But I'd rather you four check it than anyone else. I trust your eyes."

That last line is a gift. He could send anyone. He's choosing them.

## Mission 1-9 — The Still Basin
**Teaching:** Summoning
**Location:** The Still Basin (a natural spring east of the capital)

### Pre-battle

The party finds the basin. The water is real, but Tobin is unsettled by it.

> **Tobin:** "It's not wrong like the deer. It's... old. Like it's been here longer than the rock."

No enemies. Instead, the basin reacts to Hale's presence. A faint resonance — nothing dramatic, nothing explained. The water glows briefly.

**This is the game's summoning tutorial.** The Still Basin is the in-world location for summoning. It shows faces, possibilities, echoes of people who might join the guild. The lore framing: the basin reflects potential — people connected to the guild's purpose who haven't arrived yet.

> **Cinnia:** "I think it's showing you... people?"
> **Tobin:** "Reflections. But they're not here yet."

**Tutorial prompt:** The player performs their first summon. They receive a guaranteed unit (could be predetermined or from a starter pool — tuning decision).

### Post-battle (no battle — discovery scene)

The party returns to the guild. Hale has something to report, but Aldric already knows about the basin.

> **Aldric:** "The basin showed you people. Faces."
> (He's quiet for a moment. Then he smiles, and it's real this time.)
> **Aldric:** "Good. That's good. More hands for the guild means more people I get to cook for, according to Cinnia."
> **Aldric:** "...And more people we can protect."

The second line lands soft. He means Vessia. He means them. He means everyone.

## Mission 1-10 — Charter Day
**Teaching:** Chapter trial (first difficulty check)
**Location:** A road toward the frontier

### Pre-battle

The chapter's final mission. Aldric sends the party on their first proper frontier assignment: investigate a village that went quiet three days ago. This is not a training exercise.

He briefs them at the garrison table, map spread out. His tone is still warm, but there's a steadiness to it that's new.

> **Aldric:** "There's a village out past the eastern road. Hasn't sent a supply request in three days. Could be nothing. A washed-out bridge, a lazy courier."
> **Aldric:** "But three days is three days, and I'd like to know why."

He looks at each of them in turn. Cinnia. Tobin. Hearthgar. Hale.

> **Aldric:** "This isn't the training yard. You're ready, or I wouldn't be sending you."
> **Aldric:** "Come back safe. All of you."

He says it simply. Like a father seeing his kids off to school. On first playthrough, it's just a kind sendoff. After Ch. 7, it's unbearable.

The road is longer. The music is simpler. The party talks on the way.

> **Cinnia:** "I packed extra. Three days' worth."
> **Hearthgar:** "Three days? He said it's a day trip."
> **Cinnia:** "I always pack extra."
> **Tobin:** "The water says she's right to."

They don't reach the village. On the road, more proto-Hollowed creatures — a larger group, more aggressive, more wrong. Something is pushing them out of the frontier and toward the capital.

### Battle

Chapter trial: a multi-wave fight against proto-Hollowed creatures of mixed elements. The hardest fight so far. Tests everything the player has learned: party formation, Arts gauge management, elemental matchups, Skills/Arts/Bursts.

### Post-battle

The creatures are dealt with, but the road ahead is worse. The village is still a day out. Aldric recalls them via messenger bird before they get further.

> **Aldric's message:** "Come back. I'm sending a senior team."

The party returns. The chapter ends at the guild hall. Evening. The same warm light as Mission 1-1.

Cinnia is cooking. Tobin is reading the basin water in a cup. Hearthgar is cleaning the suit's joints, which is as close to comfortable as he gets.

Aldric finds Hale on the guild hall steps. Sits down next to him. Doesn't talk for a moment. Then:

> **Aldric:** "You know what I like about this job?"
> (He doesn't wait for an answer.)
> **Aldric:** "Every person who walks through that door, I get to watch them figure out they're better than they thought. Cinnia was just a cook when she started. Tobin was a kid everyone else gave up on. Hearthgar couldn't look anyone in the eye."
> **Aldric:** "You walked in quiet. You'll walk out quiet too, I expect. But you're one of us now, Hale. That means something."
> **Hale:** "...Thank you, Aldric."

His second and final line in the chapter. First time he uses anyone's name. Earned.

Aldric claps him on the shoulder, stands, and heads inside. The guild hall glows behind him.

**Chapter 1 ends.** The guild is home. The frontier is wrong. The story is moving.
