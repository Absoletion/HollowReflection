# Chapter 4 — Greywick
## Scene-by-Scene Outline · DRAFT v0.1

**Tone:** Dread through absence. Greywick is not a battlefield. It's a place where life stopped. The horror is how ordinary everything looks, minus the life. This chapter turns the cozy warmth of Ch. 1 inside out.
**Format:** Backdrop + portrait dialogue. Dialogue gets sparser as the chapter progresses. By Mission 4-6, the environment is doing most of the storytelling.
**Hale:** Two words. "I'm fine." (He isn't.)

### Cast

- **Hale, Cinnia, Tobin, Hearthgar** — Core four.
- **Marlowe, Brant** — From Ch. 2. Their comedy has been thinning since Ch. 3. Here it dies.
- **Milla** — From Ch. 2 (not guild). Guided them here. Now she has to see what her roads were leading to.
- **Garrick** — Recruited mid-chapter. Part of Aldric's advance team. Found injured, alone, holding on by stubbornness. The frontier's best hunter, and for the first time in his life, he ran. *(Moved from original Ch. 4 recruit per restructure.)*
- **Brigga** — Recruited mid-chapter. Independent demolitionist. Came to Greywick on her own to study unusual crystalline formations she'd heard about. Treating an apocalypse like a geological survey. *(Moved from original Ch. 2 recruit per restructure.)*
- **Wren** — NPC. Young shepherd's apprentice from Greywick. Apprenticed to the herdsman who becomes the Lowing Man (Ch. 7). The one person Hale saves. The one survivor.
- **The Glasswright** — First appearance. To the party: a villain who murders a village. To himself: a surgeon performing an amputation. The truth is Ch. 10's problem.
- **The Hollow Mirror** — Cameo only. A figure where a figure shouldn't be.
- **Aldric** — Absent. Garrick's account is all the party gets. He went deeper. He didn't come back.

### Narrative Arc

Chapter 4 is a one-way door. Everything before it is the world as it was. Everything after is the world as it is. The party came to find Aldric. They find something worse: proof that the wrongness they walked through in Ch. 3 isn't the disease. It's the symptom. Greywick is the wound. And the man who arrives to deal with it does so by killing the patient.

The structure mirrors a descent into the village itself. Each mission goes deeper, each layer is worse, and when the Glasswright arrives at the end, the player has been soaking in dread long enough that the explosion feels earned.

## Mission 4-1 — The Silence
**Teaching:** Hollowed enemies (different behavior from proto-Hollowed)
**Location:** Greywick village outskirts, the pastures

### Pre-battle

The party descends from the ridge into the pastures. Up close, the wrongness the road hinted at is fully realized.

The cattle are standing. All of them. Not grazing, not moving. Standing. Facing the same direction. They've been standing like that for days. Their edges are faintly translucent, like the trees on the road.

One cow is still breathing. Shallow. Mechanical. Its eyes are open and see nothing.

> **Hearthgar:** "Are they... alive?"
> **Tobin:** "I don't know what they are."

No people visible. No sound from the village. The frozen smoke from the chimney is still there, unchanged since they saw it from the ridge. A column of smoke that isn't moving.

### Battle

First fully Hollowed fauna. Not proto-Hollowed. Translucent, wrong, aggressive. They were once livestock. Now they're something the Mirror tried to copy and got wrong. They charge at odd angles. Their movements repeat, like a reflection playing back on loop.

Teaching moment: Hollowed enemies behave differently from proto-Hollowed. Their patterns repeat, they resist certain debuffs, and their stats shift mid-fight as the "reflection" adjusts.

### Post-battle

The party stands in the pasture. The standing cattle haven't moved. The Hollowed ones came from elsewhere: the ones that "woke up wrong."

> **Cinnia:** "Something did this to them. Something that doesn't understand what they are."
> **Tobin:** "Or something that understands exactly what they are and wanted to see if it could make another one."

Cinnia's cooking hands are shaking. She puts them in her pockets.

## Mission 4-2 — The Advance Camp
**Teaching:** Party composition against Hollowed enemy types
**Location:** Treeline north of Greywick, Aldric's team's camp

### Pre-battle

Milla spots it first. Boot prints. Guild-standard. Recent.

The camp is in the treeline north of the village. Three tents, one collapsed. A fire pit with cold ashes. Guild supply crates, half unpacked. Weapons and gear scattered. Not looted, just dropped.

> **Marlowe:** "This was organized. Look: supply rotation, watch schedule marked on the tree. Standard guild fieldwork."
> **Cinnia:** "Then why is it empty?"

Signs of a fight. Deep gouges in the ground. An axe buried in a tree, too high for a normal swing. Blood on the grass, dried brown. No bodies.

> **Brant:** (Examining the axe in the tree) "This is a greataxe cut. Full force."
> **Milla:** "Garrick's."

### Battle

Hollowed fauna emerge from the treeline. The camp disturbed something. The fight happens among the scattered tents and supply crates.

### Post-battle

Cinnia finds Aldric's command journal in one of the tents. The last entry is clipped, mid-sentence:

> *"Day 3. Village is worse than reports. Livestock fully compromised. Civilian population unresponsive — alive but not present. Recommend immediate evacuation of remaining—"*

Nothing after that. The pen is on the ground next to the journal.

> **Cinnia:** "Day three. He made it three days."
> **Hearthgar:** "Where did they go?"
> **Milla:** "Deeper. The boot prints go toward the village center."

## Mission 4-3 — Garrick
**Teaching:** Rescue combat (protect an NPC unit during the fight)
**Location:** Greywick's eastern quarter, a collapsed farmhouse

### Pre-battle

A sound. Metal on something that isn't metal. The party follows it through the empty streets.

They find Garrick in the rubble of a collapsed farmhouse. He's propped against a wall, greataxe in one hand, the other arm hanging wrong. He's surrounded by shattered Hollowed. He's been killing them as they come. For days.

> **Garrick:** "...Took you long enough."

He's in bad shape. Deep cuts, exhaustion, and something worse: his left arm has a faint translucent patch near the shoulder. He's been in proximity to the Hollowing long enough that it's started on him.

> **Cinnia:** "Your arm—"
> **Garrick:** "I know. Get to it later. They come in waves. Every few hours."

### Battle

The next wave hits while Cinnia is treating Garrick. Rescue combat: protect Garrick (reduced HP NPC ally) while fighting off Hollowed. He fights from his position with limited attacks. Not helpless, just damaged.

### Post-battle

Cinnia treats the translucent patch on Garrick's arm. It doesn't respond to healing normally. She can slow it, not reverse it. First hint that Hollowing can't simply be healed.

Garrick gives his account. Exhausted. Blunt.

> **Garrick:** "We got here on day two. Village was already like this. Aldric took one look and said we weren't dealing with a plague. Something had been here."
> **Garrick:** "Night of day three, they came. The Hollowed. Not from outside. From the village itself. The livestock, the buildings, the damned cobblestones. Everything we were standing on started waking up wrong."
> **Garrick:** "Aldric ordered fallback to the treeline. Most of the team made it. I got cut off."
> **Cinnia:** "Where did Aldric go?"
> **Garrick:** "Deeper. He saw something in the village center. Wouldn't say what. Told us to fall back and went in alone."

Beat.

> **Garrick:** "That was seven days ago."
> **Cinnia:** "And you stayed."
> **Garrick:** "I know the herdsman here. Oren. Knew him thirty years. Taught his apprentice to mend fences." (He looks at the frozen livestock in the distance.) "Couldn't leave without knowing."

Garrick joins the party. His arm is partially translucent, but he can fight. He doesn't mention the arm again.

## Mission 4-4 — The Geologist
**Teaching:** Break mechanics introduction (Brigga's specialty)
**Location:** Greywick's market square, a Hollowglass formation

### Pre-battle

The party moves deeper. The streets are intact but empty. Doors open. Food on tables, untouched. A child's toy on a doorstep.

In the market square: a large formation of Hollowglass. Crystallized material that looks like frozen water but reflects nothing. Taller than a person. Several clustered together like a growth.

And sitting cross-legged in front of one, tapping it with a rock pick and taking notes, is a squat dwarf woman with scorched orange hair and a powder-keg maul leaning against the crystal.

> **Brigga:** (Without looking up) "Oi. You lot step left. You're in my light."
> **Marlowe:** "...There's a woman casually studying the apocalypse."
> **Brigga:** "It's not an apocalypse, it's a crystalline lattice with a propagation pattern I've never seen. And I've seen a lot of lattices."

She's been here for two days. Arrived independently after hearing reports of "unusual mineral formations" near Greywick. The horror doesn't bother her. The crystal fascinates her.

> **Cinnia:** "How are you alive? The Hollowed—"
> **Brigga:** "Oh, those things? Three so far. They don't like the sound the maul makes on the glass." (She taps the Hollowglass. It rings, high and wrong.) "See? They scatter. Animals don't like dissonance."
> **Brigga:** "Anyway. This stuff isn't natural crystal. It's a copy of crystal. Like something saw quartz once and tried to build it from memory. The angles are wrong by about two degrees. And it doesn't reflect."

### Battle

A larger wave of Hollowed interrupts. Brigga picks up her maul with one hand. She hits hard and she hits to break things.

Teaching: Break mechanics. Brigga's attacks deal break damage to enemies' stagger bars. When the bar breaks, the enemy is stunned and takes increased damage. First introduction of the system.

> **Brigga:** (Mid-fight, mid-swing) "SEE THE FAULT LINE? HIT THERE!"

### Post-battle

Brigga joins the party. She's not a guild member and doesn't plan to be. She's here because the glass is interesting. But interesting things that kill people tend to need solving, and she's got the maul for it.

> **Brigga:** "I'm not joining your club. I'm going the same direction. There's a difference."
> **Marlowe:** "Noted."

## Mission 4-5 — Following the Glass
**Teaching:** Elemental interactions with Hollowed terrain
**Location:** Greywick's inner streets, toward the village center

### Pre-battle

Brigga reads the Hollowglass formations like a geologist reads strata. She points out the propagation pattern.

> **Brigga:** "It's not random. Look, the crystal faces all point inward. Whatever made this started at the center and grew out. Or..."
> **Brigga:** "Or something at the center is still making it."

The deeper they go, the worse it gets. Buildings with walls going translucent. A well where the water has crystallized mid-splash. A dog frozen mid-bark, its edges glinting.

Garrick stops at one house. Stares at the door.

> **Garrick:** "Oren's house."

He doesn't go in. He doesn't need to. Through the translucent wall, they can see a shape inside. Motionless. Standing.

> **Garrick:** (Quietly) "...I'm sorry, old friend."

He walks on.

### Battle

Stronger Hollowed in the inner streets. The Hollowglass formations create terrain effects: resonance zones that debuff the party, crystal patches that empower Hollowed enemies. The fight is harder and more environmental than previous ones.

### Post-battle

The party reaches a small square near the center. A girl is sitting on a stone bench. Young, maybe sixteen. She's not translucent. She's not frozen. She's alive, hugging her knees, staring at nothing.

> **Cinnia:** "Hey. Can you hear me?"

The girl flinches. Looks up. Her eyes take a moment to focus.

> **Wren:** "...You're real?"
> **Cinnia:** "We're real. We're from the guild. What's your name?"
> **Wren:** "Wren. I was... I'm Oren's apprentice. The herdsman. I was with the flock when it—" (She stops.) "They stopped moving. All at once. And then Oren stopped too."

She's been hiding in the village for over a week, surviving on stored food, avoiding the Hollowed. She saw Aldric's team arrive. She saw them leave. She saw Aldric go toward the center alone.

> **Wren:** "He went toward the big glass. The one that hums. I tried to tell him not to, but he couldn't hear me. Or he didn't stop."

Garrick kneels next to her.

> **Garrick:** "I knew your master, girl. I taught you to mend fences three summers ago. You remember?"
> **Wren:** (A ghost of recognition) "...Mr. Garrick?"
> **Garrick:** "Come on. Stay with us."

## Mission 4-6 — The Center
**Teaching:** Boss-tier Hollowed encounters
**Location:** Greywick's central square, the largest Hollowglass formation

### Pre-battle

The village center. A fountain, dry. The guild hall. A church. All normal architecture, all going translucent.

And in the middle of the square: a Hollowglass formation the size of a house. It hums. Not a sound. A vibration the party feels in their teeth. Brigga's instruments are going haywire.

> **Brigga:** "That's not a formation. That's a focal point. Everything in this village is growing from THAT."

Tobin checks his water. It shows the square. The formation. And behind the formation, standing perfectly still, a figure.

> **Tobin:** "Someone's there."

He looks up. The square is empty. He looks at the water. The figure is closer.

> **Tobin:** "Cinnia."
> **Cinnia:** "What?"
> **Tobin:** "Don't look at the water. Just... don't."

He pours out the vial. His hands are shaking. He doesn't explain. (The Hollow Mirror's first cameo. No one else sees it. Tobin will not talk about this until Ch. 6.)

### Battle

The Hollowglass formation pulses. The strongest Hollowed yet emerge: partially crystalline creatures that reform when shattered. Boss-tier fight without a named boss. The Hollowed are stronger, faster, more coordinated, as if something is directing them.

### Post-battle

The formation is still humming. Whatever is inside it, they can't reach it. Brigga's analysis:

> **Brigga:** "I could crack it. But I don't know what's inside. Could be the source. Could be what's keeping it contained. I'd rather know before I swing."

The party debates what to do. Garrick wants to push deeper. Aldric came this way. Cinnia wants to pull back and regroup. Milla is mapping escape routes.

The argument is interrupted.

## Mission 4-7 — The Crystallization
**Teaching:** None. Story climax. Scripted loss.
**Location:** Greywick's central square

### The Glasswright arrives.

He doesn't announce himself. One moment the square holds only the party. The next, a man is standing on top of the central formation.

Tall. Gaunt. A coat that might have been fine once, now threadbare. Hands stained with something that catches light wrong: Hollowglass, embedded in his skin. He looks tired. He looks like he's been doing this for a very long time.

He doesn't look at the party. He looks at the formation. He puts one hand on it.

> **Cinnia:** "Who are you? What are you—"

The Hollowglass begins to spread. Not from the formation. From HIM. From his hand, outward, in every direction. Fast. Glass racing across the cobblestones, up the walls, over the buildings. The translucent village begins to crystallize completely. Wrong becomes frozen becomes glass.

> **Marlowe:** "EVERYONE MOVE!"

### Scripted loss fight.

The party engages the Glasswright. He fights with blades forged from his own body: Hollowglass edges that he pulls from his arms, his ribs, his spine. Every blade costs him. Every swing leaves him thinner.

This is a scripted loss. The party cannot win. The Glasswright is operating at a power level far beyond them. The fight exists to show the player what they're up against and to trigger Hale's scar moment.

He doesn't fight to kill. He fights to finish the crystallization without interference. He deflects. He redirects. He puts the party down efficiently, without cruelty, without anger. Like a surgeon brushing away someone who walked into the operating room.

### The Scar.

The crystallization wave reaches the bench where Wren is hiding. She's frozen by terror. The glass is racing toward her.

Hale moves. No hesitation, no announcement. He throws himself between Wren and the wave. The Hollowglass hits him directly across the left side of his neck and shoulder.

It should crystallize him. It doesn't. The glass hits his skin and cracks. Fragments fall away, leaving a raw, bleeding wound where the crystal tried to take root and failed. The scar.

The Glasswright stops. Everything stops. The crystallization pauses.

He's staring at Hale. Not at the wound. At the place where the glass broke. His expression shifts. Not surprise, not fear. Recognition. Like he's seen this before. Once. In a mirror.

The Glasswright says nothing. He looks at Hale for a long moment. Then he turns, walks into the glass, and is gone.

The crystallization resumes without him, slower now. The party has minutes, not seconds.

### Retreat.

> **Cinnia:** "We need to go. NOW."
> **Garrick:** (Looking toward the center, toward where Aldric went) "But—"
> **Cinnia:** "Garrick. We go NOW or we don't go."

Garrick looks at the glass consuming the village. At Oren's house, now fully crystal. At Wren, shaking, alive only because of Hale.

He goes.

The party runs. Milla leads. She knows these roads, even these roads. Wren is carried by Brant. Hale is bleeding from the neck, one hand pressed to the wound, running.

They clear the village edge as the crystallization finishes behind them. Greywick is glass. Every building, every street, every frozen cow, every stopped clock. Glass that doesn't reflect.

The party stops on the ridge where they first saw the village. Below them: a crystal graveyard.

Cinnia turns to Hale. His hand is on his neck. Blood between his fingers.

> **Cinnia:** "Let me see."

He shows her. The wound is deep but not life-threatening. The skin around it has a faint crystalline texture. Not spreading. Just there. A mark.

> **Cinnia:** "Hale. What happened? The glass hit you and it... broke."
> **Hale:** "I'm fine."

Two words. He's lying. The scar will ache near Hollowed things for the rest of the game. He doesn't know that yet. Neither does the player.

Cinnia heals the wound. The scar stays. It will always stay.

### Chapter end.

The party on the ridge. Seven guild members, one courier, one demolitionist, one shepherd girl with nothing left to go back to. Below them, a village made of glass.

No one says "what do we do now." They know. They go back. They report. They tell Bessa she was right, and that it doesn't matter, because what they found is worse than what she feared.

Garrick takes one last look at Greywick. At Oren's house.

He doesn't say the name.

**Chapter 4 ends.**

## Pacing Note

Seven missions. The chapter is denser than Ch. 3 ("The Road") in both combat and story. Five of seven missions have real combat, escalating from Hollowed livestock to boss-tier crystalline enemies. The Glasswright's appearance in 4-7 is the first scripted loss in the game. The player needs to feel the gap between their power level and his. The fight should last long enough to be dramatic but should never feel winnable.

This chapter recruits two units (Garrick, Brigga) and introduces one major NPC (Wren) and two major antagonists (the Glasswright, the Hollow Mirror via cameo). That's dense. Garrick is the emotional anchor (his connection to Oren), Brigga is the analytical voice (her read on the glass), and Wren is the human cost. Each serves a different function. They shouldn't overlap.

## Restructure Note

With the journey split into its own chapter (Ch. 3 "The Road"), the chapter numbering shifts forward by one from the original design doc. The Glasswright reveal and Hale's scar payoffs (originally Ch. 9 and the exam) should be renumbered accordingly in future outlines. The 10-chapter cap is no longer enforced; the story takes the space it needs.
