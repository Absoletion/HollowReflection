# Katie — 4★ Character Reference

## Core identity

- Element: Dark
- Role: Defender / Tank
- Rarity: 4★ base form
- Evolution: none designated yet
- Epithet: the Night Radiographer

Katie is dry, intensely private, artistic, grumpy, hyperintelligent, and openly resistant to unnecessary physical contact. She loves cats and radiology, but neither interest should reduce her to a novelty character. Her humor is sparse and usually delivered without changing expression.

## Visual language

- Black hair in a practical loose bun with long face-framing strands.
- Rectangular glasses and cool cyan eyes.
- Heavy asymmetric dark plate armor over a deep violet tabard.
- Skull, cat, and clinical-imaging motifs used as small personal marks.
- Oversized two-handed radiographic hammer: a dark metal imaging chamber containing a cyan skeletal cat projection.
- Dark-violet energy arcs paired with clinical cyan image light.
- Brushes and imaging vials hang from the weapon as signs of her artistic and technical work.

## Personality direction

Katie speaks precisely and economically. She rarely raises her voice. Irritation appears as narrower wording, longer pauses, or a flat correction rather than shouting. She dislikes being touched and establishes physical boundaries early. Cats receive substantially more patience than people.

## Combat concept

Katie controls space through diagnostic barriers and threat mapping. She does not tank by acting boisterous; enemies are redirected toward her because she identifies attack vectors, marks safe boundaries, and makes herself the least penetrable point on the field.

- Passive — Lead Apron: begins battle with a personal shield.
- Skill — Personal Space: taunt, shield, and temporary DEF up.
- Art — Contrast Study: reveals enemy intents and shields the party.
- Burst — CAT Scan: heavy party shield, party DEF up, and enemy ATK down.

“CAT Scan” is intentional dry wordplay, but the animation and writing should remain visually credible: a spectral feline skeleton passes through a diagnostic field while enemy attack vectors are exposed.
