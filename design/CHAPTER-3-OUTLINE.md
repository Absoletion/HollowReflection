# Chapter 3 — The Road
## Scene-by-Scene Outline · DRAFT v0.1

**Tone:** Quiet tension under forced normalcy. The party is traveling together for the first time as a full group, and the road is slowly teaching them that the world they left is not the world ahead. The horror here is gradual. Things don't attack. They just stop.
**Format:** Backdrop + portrait dialogue. This is a character chapter. Banter-heavy early, thinning out as the road worsens.
**Hale:** Two words. "Keep moving." (When the group hesitates at what they find.)

### Cast

- **Hale, Cinnia, Tobin, Hearthgar** — Core four. This chapter deepens the dynamics established in Ch. 1.
- **Marlowe & Brant** — First time traveling with the group long-term. Their banter covers nerves. Marlowe's humor gets quieter as the road goes wrong. Brant just stops talking.
- **Milla** — The guide. She knows every mile of this road. Watching it change in real time is personal for her. This is her professional identity dissolving under her feet.
- **Wren** — Not yet encountered (Ch. 4).
- **Celia** — Brief appearance only. Encountered on the road heading the opposite direction. Not recruited (Ch. 5), but this is the first meeting. She's traveling alone.

### Narrative Arc

Chapter 3 is a road trip through a world that's forgetting how to work. The first half is about the party learning to function as a unit. People who've only known each other for days, forced into close quarters on a march toward something none of them can name. The second half is about what the road shows them: the wrongness isn't just at Greywick. It's spreading. It's on the road. It's in the silence between towns.

The chapter's job is to earn Greywick. When the party arrives in Ch. 4, the player should already feel the dread in their stomach because the road built it mile by mile.

## Mission 3-1 — The March
**Teaching:** Formation/party order (if applicable to combat system, otherwise light combat refresher)
**Location:** Eastern road, first day out of the capital

### Pre-battle

Morning. The party moves through the eastern gate. The capital shrinks behind them. Milla takes point without being asked. She knows this road.

The first stretch is normal. Green fields, working farms, a merchant cart heading toward the city. Ordinary. The kind of road that makes you wonder what you were worried about.

> **Marlowe:** "So. How does this work? Do we march in formation? Do I call you 'sir'?" (To Hale.)
> **Hearthgar:** "He won't answer that."
> **Marlowe:** "Noted. Do YOU outrank me?"
> **Hearthgar:** "I am a lantern. I do not have a rank."
> **Marlowe:** "...Right."

The party settles into a travel rhythm. Cinnia walks near Tobin. Hearthgar's bulk makes him a natural rearguard. Brant carries most of the heavy supplies without being asked.

> **Cinnia:** "Milla, how long to Greywick on foot?"
> **Milla:** "Three days if the road holds. Four if the weather doesn't cooperate. Five if whatever happened to the road past the crossroads has gotten worse."
> **Cinnia:** "And if it has?"
> **Milla:** "Then I'll tell you when we get there."

### Battle

Bandits. Not Hollowed, not proto-Hollowed. Just people. Desperate ones, from the look of them. They picked the wrong group.

This is a palate cleanser. A normal fight to remind the player what normal enemies feel like before the chapter takes that away.

### Post-battle

The bandits scatter. One drops a bag of stolen goods. Brant picks it up, looks inside, and hands it to Marlowe.

> **Marlowe:** "Grain. They were stealing grain."
> **Milla:** "Grain shipments from the east have been thin for weeks. People notice."
> **Tobin:** "The east. Where we're going."
> **Milla:** "Where we're going."

## Mission 3-2 — Camp
**Teaching:** None. Character chapter. (If a gameplay hook is needed: equipment management tutorial.)
**Location:** Campsite, first night on the road

### No battle. Story only.

Evening. The party makes camp off the road. First night as a group.

Cinnia cooks. Of course she does. But this isn't stress-cooking. She's feeding people she barely knows, and the act of it is her way of making them hers.

> **Cinnia:** (Handing a bowl to Brant) "You eat meat?"
> **Brant:** "Yes."
> **Cinnia:** "Good. You don't get a choice anyway. Eat."
> **Brant:** (Takes the bowl. Pauses.) "...Thank you."

Marlowe is charming his way through the group. Testing the waters. He's a talker, and talkers need to know who'll talk back.

> **Marlowe:** (To Hearthgar) "So what's the story? Lantern, guild, big metal man. How'd that happen?"
> **Hearthgar:** "I walked into the guild hall. I asked to join. Aldric said yes."
> **Marlowe:** "That's it? No dramatic origin? No tragic backstory?"
> **Hearthgar:** "I was lonely."

Beat. Marlowe didn't expect that.

> **Marlowe:** "...Fair enough."

Tobin is sitting apart, checking his water. Quietly. Cinnia brings him a bowl without asking.

> **Cinnia:** "Anything?"
> **Tobin:** "The water shows the road ahead. It looks... normal. But the reading feels thin. Like the water's guessing instead of seeing."
> **Cinnia:** "What does that mean?"
> **Tobin:** "I don't know yet."

Milla is maintaining her boots. Courier habit. She doesn't join the fireside conversation, but she's listening.

Hale is on watch. Sitting on a rock at the edge of the firelight. Not sleeping.

> **Hearthgar:** (Quietly, to Cinnia) "Does he ever sleep?"
> **Cinnia:** "Not where you can see him."

## Mission 3-3 — The Waystation
**Teaching:** Supply management (if applicable) or environmental storytelling
**Location:** A courier waystation on the eastern road, day two

### Pre-battle

Midmorning, day two. Milla leads them to a waystation she's used a hundred times. A small stone building with a water pump, a resupply shelf, and a logbook for couriers to mark their passage.

It's empty. That's normal. Waystations aren't staffed. But the logbook tells a story.

> **Milla:** (Flipping pages) "Last entry is three weeks ago. That's mine. Before me... two weeks before that. Before that, regular traffic, every few days."

She looks at the resupply shelf. Full. Nobody's taken supplies in three weeks. The water pump works but the water comes out slightly wrong. Clear, but it doesn't splash right.

> **Milla:** "This station services four courier routes. I was the last person to use it."
> **Marlowe:** "Maybe the others took different roads."
> **Milla:** "There are no different roads. This IS the road."

### Battle

Proto-Hollowed on the road beyond the waystation. The party has crossed into the zone where the wrongness begins. These are weaker than what they'll face later, but they're HERE, on a road that was safe three weeks ago. The frontier is moving.

### Post-battle

> **Cinnia:** "Proto-Hollowed. On a courier road. This isn't Greywick. This is the road TO Greywick."
> **Garrick's words from Ch. 4 echo forward here: the Hollowed aren't staying put. They're spreading.**
> **Milla:** "The merchant with the bad jerky. He set up at the halfway marker, every week, for six years. He's not here."
> **Brant:** "Maybe he moved."
> **Milla:** "Maybe."

She doesn't believe it. Neither does the player.

## Mission 3-4 — The Opposite Direction
**Teaching:** NPC encounters / world-building
**Location:** Eastern road, day two, afternoon

### Pre-battle

The party crests a hill and sees someone on the road below. Coming toward them. Moving fast. On foot.

It's a woman. Road-worn dress with festival ribbons sewn over patches. A lyre across her back. She's not running, but she's not strolling either. She's walking with purpose, away from the east.

> **Cinnia:** "Hey! Are you alright?"
> **Celia:** (Slowing, wary) "...You're heading east?"
> **Cinnia:** "To Greywick."
> **Celia:** "Then no, I'm not alright, because you're making a terrible decision and I just came FROM a terrible decision."

Celia. Traveling songstress. She'd been performing at inns along the frontier. Two weeks ago, the inns started closing. Not shutting down. Just... stopping. Doors open, fires cold, nobody inside.

> **Celia:** "I played the Greywick inn six weeks ago. Full house. Lovely crowd. I was supposed to play it again last week. The inn is still standing. The door was open. The mugs were on the tables. Nobody was there."
> **Marlowe:** "Nobody?"
> **Celia:** "I didn't stay long enough to check."

She's heading west, toward the capital. She doesn't want company and she doesn't want to be recruited. Not yet. (Ch. 5.)

> **Cinnia:** "Come with us. We're guild."
> **Celia:** "I know what guild looks like. I sang for your guildmaster once. Big man, warm voice, tipped well." (A beat.) "I'd rather be moving the other direction right now. No offense."

She gives them one more thing before she goes: a song. Not a performance. Quietly, almost to herself. A few bars of the ballad from Ch. 1 (Celia's ballad, the one about the guild). But slower. Sadder. Like a lullaby for something that isn't sleeping.

> **Celia:** "If you find Greywick... come back and tell me I was wrong. I'd like to be wrong."

She walks west. The party watches her go.

### Battle

The road ahead of Celia's warning. Proto-Hollowed in higher density. The environment is shifting: colors slightly washed, sounds slightly muted. The world is getting thinner.

### Post-battle

> **Tobin:** "She said the inns just stopped. No attack. No disaster. They just... stopped."
> **Cinnia:** "Like the livestock Milla described."
> **Tobin:** "Like everything out here."

## Mission 3-5 — The Halfway Marker
**Teaching:** Hollowed enemy behavior patterns
**Location:** The halfway marker on the eastern road, day three

### Pre-battle

Day three. They reach the halfway marker. Milla has been talking about this spot: a flat stone by the road where the jerky merchant set up his cart every week. The stone is there. The merchant is not. His cart is.

The cart is on its side. The goods are scattered but not taken. An awning pole is snapped. And the jerky, still hanging from the drying rack, is faintly translucent.

> **Milla:** (Touching the cart) "His name was Haddon. He overcharged for everything and his jerky tasted like boot leather." (She pulls her hand back.) "...The wood feels wrong."
> **Hearthgar:** "Wrong how?"
> **Milla:** "Smooth. Too smooth. Like it's not wood anymore. Like something is wearing wood."

First time a party member articulates what the Hollowing actually does: it copies. It wears things.

### Battle

First full Hollowed on the road (not proto-Hollowed). These are stronger, and they behave differently: movements loop, they resist debuffs, their stats shift mid-fight. The teaching moment is learning to read their patterns.

The environment around the halfway marker has terrain effects: patches of translucent ground, muted-color zones that reduce the party's accuracy.

### Post-battle

The party sits with the realization that Hollowed creatures are on the open road, two days from the capital. This isn't contained. This isn't just Greywick.

> **Cinnia:** "Bessa said the frontier was deteriorating. She wasn't guessing."
> **Milla:** "Bessa's reports were three weeks old. This is worse than her reports."
> **Marlowe:** (Uncharacteristically quiet) "How much worse does it get?"

Nobody answers. Nobody has to. They can see the road ahead. The trees are thinning. Not losing leaves. Losing color. Losing substance.

> **Tobin:** "The water is showing me the road ahead. It's... there. But it looks like a painting of a road. Flat. No depth."
> **Cinnia:** "That's what we saw from the crossroads in Ch. 2."
> **Tobin:** "It's closer now."

## Mission 3-6 — The Last Inn
**Teaching:** Resource awareness (supply check before Greywick)
**Location:** A roadside inn, one day from Greywick

### Pre-battle

Late day three. The last inn before Greywick. Milla knows it. The sign is still hanging. The door is open.

The party enters. Tables set for dinner. Mugs half-full. A pot on the hearth, long cold, with something that used to be stew congealed at the bottom. Upstairs, beds made. Personal belongings in the rooms. A child's drawing pinned to a wall.

Nobody is here. Nobody has been here for weeks.

> **Cinnia:** (Looking at the cold stew) "They were mid-meal."
> **Marlowe:** "No signs of a fight. No blood. No damage."
> **Cinnia:** "That's worse."

Milla checks the inn's logbook.

> **Milla:** "Last guest signed in four weeks ago. The innkeeper's handwriting stops mid-word, same as Aldric's journal. Just... stops."

The party camps in the inn. It's shelter, and they need rest before Greywick. But nobody sleeps well.

Night scene. Cinnia is in the kitchen, cleaning the cold pot. Not because it needs cleaning. Because her hands need something to do.

Hearthgar finds her.

> **Hearthgar:** "You don't have to clean their kitchen."
> **Cinnia:** "Someone should."
> **Hearthgar:** "Cinnia."
> **Cinnia:** "If I don't do something with my hands, I'm going to think about what happened to the people who were eating that stew. And I can't think about that right now. So I'm cleaning."

He sits with her. Doesn't say anything else. His furnace glows low and warm. She cleans.

Separately: Milla is on the inn's porch, looking east. Tobin joins her.

> **Tobin:** "You've been quiet."
> **Milla:** "I'm counting. Every waystation, every inn, every marker. All empty. That's six stops on this road. I've run this road for four years. I knew the name of every person at every stop."
> **Tobin:** "Knew?"
> **Milla:** "Know. I know their names. Present tense. Until someone tells me otherwise."

### Battle

Dawn, day four. Proto-Hollowed have gathered around the inn overnight, drawn to the warmth of the party. Morning fight to clear the road.

### Post-battle

The party packs up. Milla takes a last look at the inn.

> **Milla:** "One more day."
> **Cinnia:** "One more day."

The road ahead is visibly wrong. Trees translucent. Grass that doesn't move. A sky that looks flat, like a painting. The same flat horizon they saw from the crossroads in Ch. 2, but now they're walking into it.

**Hale's line.** The group pauses at the inn's threshold. Looking at the road ahead. Nobody moves for a moment. Everyone can feel what's coming.

> **Hale:** "Keep moving."

Two words. Not a command. Not a rally. Just the only thing left to do.

The party moves.

## Mission 3-7 — The Edge
**Teaching:** Environmental hazards (full introduction, building on 3-5's terrain effects)
**Location:** Final stretch of road, the approach to Greywick

### Pre-battle

The last stretch. The wrongness is complete. The road is intact but the world around it has given up pretending. Trees are transparent. The creek from Milla's scouting run (2-7) is still flowing but makes no sound. The grass is the right color and the wrong texture. Birds sit on branches and don't move.

> **Brigga would love this.** *(She's not here yet. She's already at Greywick. But the player who meets her next chapter will understand why she came.)*

Brant, who's been getting progressively quieter, stops walking. Stares at a field.

> **Marlowe:** "Brant?"
> **Brant:** "The wheat."
> **Marlowe:** "What about it?"
> **Brant:** "It's growing. But it's not moving. There's wind and the wheat isn't moving."

Everyone looks. He's right. The field is full and golden and absolutely still. Wind passes through the party's hair and doesn't touch the wheat.

> **Tobin:** "It's not wheat anymore. It's a picture of wheat."
> **Milla:** "...We're close."

### Battle

Hollowed on the road. Stronger here, at the edge of Greywick's influence. Environmental hazards in full effect: translucent terrain, muted zones, resonance patches from nearby Hollowglass formations. This fight is a gear check before Ch. 4.

### Post-battle

The party crests the final ridge. Below them: Greywick.

It looks fine. Rooftops intact. Smoke from a chimney. Livestock in the fields.

> **Cinnia:** "It looks normal."
> **Milla:** "No it doesn't. Look at the livestock."

The cattle are standing. All of them. Not grazing, not moving. Standing. Facing the same direction.

The smoke from the chimney hasn't changed. It's frozen. A column of smoke that isn't moving.

The village is there. The village is wrong.

**Chapter 3 ends.** The party on the ridge. Greywick below. Whatever they came to find, it's down there.

## Pacing Note

Seven missions. Three have real combat (3-1 bandits, 3-5 Hollowed, 3-7 Hollowed), two have lighter fights (3-3 proto-Hollowed, 3-6 dawn skirmish), and two are story-only (3-2 camp, 3-4 Celia encounter). This is deliberately lighter on combat than Ch. 1 or the upcoming Ch. 4. The chapter's job is character and atmosphere, not action. The player should arrive at Greywick feeling like they walked there.

The Celia encounter (3-4) is optional in the sense that she doesn't join and the mission has minimal combat. But her appearance does three things: plants her for Ch. 5 recruitment, gives the party an outsider's account of the frontier collapsing, and recontextualizes her ballad from Ch. 1. Cutting her would save a mission but lose all three threads.

The chapter recruits no one. That's intentional. Ch. 4 picks up Garrick and Brigga. Ch. 3 is about the people the party already has, learning to trust each other on a road that's trying to teach them they shouldn't have come.
