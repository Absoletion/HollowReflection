# Hollow Reflections — Roster Codex
**Version 0.2 · Companion to the Design Doc · Red-teamed · All numbers are placeholders**

The 30 collectible units of the Vessia Adventurers' Guild. Kit template: **Basic** (free, +1⚡) · **Skill** (1⚡) · **Art** (2⚡) · **Burst** (3⚡). Simpler units have Skill + Burst only (the complexity dial). ⚡ = Energy bars.

## Global Rulings (v0.2 — added after red-team, to close systemic exploits)
1. **Break-resistance ramp:** each time a boss is staggered, its guard bar refills with +25% capacity. Break comps get 2–3 staggers per fight, never a stun-lock.
2. **One on-stagger rider per stagger:** if multiple on-stagger effects are fielded (Brigga's Grand Opening, Borin's extend, Nyx's re-act), the player picks ONE to resolve.
3. **Mitigation cap:** ability-based damage reduction stacks to a hard cap of 50%. Guard applies after the cap (Guard stays sacred; stacking doesn't).
4. **Delay rule:** a boss can be delayed only once between each of its actions. No delay-chaining.
5. **Stall Tax enforcement:** every boss's stall tax must be at least partially unmitigatable and not a damage-over-time (so no roster passive can opt out of the pillar).

**Connective tissue — Ansel the Unbroken (NPC, proposed):** the guild's living legend, built up through Ch. 1–5 as mentor, recruiter, and morale anchor, then taken by the Mirror as **Boss 2** (Ch. 7). Several backstories below hook into him deliberately. He is NOT collectible (protects the grid). Pending Tra's approval.

## 🔥 FIRE

### Kaizan — Attacker · *"One draw. Again."*
**Appearance:** Lean ronin, crimson haori worn off one shoulder, high black ponytail streaked gray, an odachi too long for its sheath. His idle sprite never takes his hand off the hilt.
**Story:** A duelist from the eastern isles chasing the "perfect draw" — one cut so complete it ends a fight before it begins. Joined the guild because Vessia's monsters don't bow out of duels. Treats Hale's awakening as a personal insult until Hale beats him in a sparring scene (Ch. 8 sidequest — kept OUT of the Ch. 7 grief chapter), after which he's insufferably loyal. Recruit: Ch. 4.
**Kit (2 specials — simple, fast):**
- **Passive — Battle Rhythm:** his flurry basic charges **+2⚡** instead of +1. Fastest unit to Burst in the game.
- **Skill — Draw Cut (1⚡):** instant heavy strike; +50% damage if an enemy damaged Kaizan since his last action.
- **Burst — Hundred Petal Draw (3⚡):** the game's highest hit-count attack; sheathes, then the screen fills with petals and cuts.

### Brigga — Breaker · *"Stand back. Or don't, I'm not your mum."*
**Appearance:** Squat dwarf woman, soot-blacked cheeks, orange hair in scorched twin buns, leather apron full of fuses, dragging a powder-keg maul twice her size.
**Story:** Ex-mining-corps demolitionist, discharged for "excessive enthusiasm." The guild is the only outfit that lets her blow things up legally. Reads Hollowglass fractures like a geologist — her Ch. 5 mission establishes that hollowed material has fault lines, foreshadowing the break system's lore. Recruit: Ch. 2.
**Kit (3 specials):**
- **Passive — Short Fuse:** +25% break damage while any enemy's break bar is above half (she loves fresh walls).
- **Skill — Kegcracker (1⚡):** strike that ignores a chunk of the guard bar outright.
- **Art — Blast Mining (2⚡):** AoE break damage; hits adds too.
- **Burst — Grand Opening (3⚡):** massive break hit; if it triggers the stagger, the party's Energy all +1 (the door's open, everyone in).

### Hearthgar — Defender · *"S-stay behind me! The armor means it, even if I don't sound like it!"*
**Appearance:** A hulking ember-golem suit in matte BLACK iron, deep-red furnace glow through the cracks — with a scrawny teenager's nervous eyes visible through the visor slit. The suit's sprite towers; his voice bubble is tiny. (Palette pushed hot/dark to contrast Lumen's cool gold.)
**Story:** An orphan who crawled into a derelict furnace-golem to sleep and woke up bonded to it. The guild took him in when no one else would house a boy in a two-ton fire hazard. Ansel taught him that being scared and standing anyway is the whole job. His Ch. 7 grief scene after Boss 2 is the roster's emotional low point. Recruit: Ch. 3.
**Kit (3 specials):**
- **Passive — Stoked:** each hit Hearthgar takes adds a **Cinder** stack (max 5), raising his DEF slightly per stack.
- **Skill — Brace the Hearth (1⚡):** taunt for 1 turn.
- **Art — Bank the Coals (2⚡):** consume all Cinder stacks; shield the PARTY, scaling per stack. (He converts pain into protection — the sponge-to-shield unit, distinct from Galvan's pain-into-damage engine and from Lumen's small automatic shields.)
- **Burst — Open the Furnace (3⚡):** taunt all enemies + retaliate on every hit taken this turn.

### Cinnia — Healer · *"Nobody fights hollow on an empty stomach."*
**Appearance (4★ canon):** Bright green eyes, warm freckles, and vivid red hair in high curled twin-tails with small braids, wheat ornaments, and apple-shaped clasps. Her cream-yellow field dress is reinforced by a brown leather corset-harness, bracers, and practical plated boots, with orange flame, wheat, and apple embroidery around the hem. Her evolved weapon is a long-handled blackened-iron frying pan used as both casting focus and defensive implement; its engraved apple-flame emblem burns when she channels Fire. The silhouette stays soft, warm, and immediately distinct from the roster's armored fighters.
**Personality:** Cheerful, nurturing, stubborn, and endearingly ditzy. Cinnia misplaces utensils she is already holding, loses the thread of conversations, and occasionally prepares for the wrong problem—but becomes startlingly precise when someone is hungry, hurt, or in danger. Her absent-mindedness creates warmth and comedy without making her incompetent; cooking, field medicine, and protecting the guild are the areas where her instincts are exceptional.
**Story:** The guild's cook long before she was a combatant — she got field-certified out of stubbornness when recruits kept getting hurt too far from her kitchen. Her Ch. 1 stew scene is the game's first warm moment, which makes Greywick land harder. She may forget the name of a supply or why she entered a room, but remembers every recruit's allergies, favorite meal, and old injuries. Recruit: Ch. 1 (starter).
**Kit (3 specials):**
- **Passive — Seconds:** any overheal from her abilities becomes **Well-Fed** (small regen, 2 turns).
- **Skill — Quick Bite (1⚡):** single-ally heal + Well-Fed.
- **Art — Field Rations (2⚡):** party heal.
- **Burst — Full-Course Feast (3⚡):** big party heal + party ATK up (morale is a nutrient).

### Rosso — Supporter · *"Louder! The dead parts of the world should hear us coming!"*
**Appearance:** Flamboyant bard, plumed hat perpetually singed, crimson coat with powder burns, a lute with rocket tubes built into the body.
**Story:** A festival pyrotechnician whose hometown cancelled its festivals after the news of Greywick spread. He joined the guild to give people reasons to celebrate again, and treats every boss kill as a show finale. Writes an in-game ballad about the party that updates per chapter. Recruit: Ch. 4.
**Kit (3 specials):**
- **Passive — Showtime:** buffs Rosso applies last +1 turn.
- **Skill — Fanfare (1⚡):** ATK up, one ally.
- **Art — Pyro Chorus (2⚡):** ATK up, party.
- **Burst — Grand Finale (3⚡):** party attacks gain an ADDED Fire component (does not replace native elements — never a trap vs. Water bosses) + ATK up (stacks with Pyro Chorus per Showtime).

## 💧 WATER

### Marlowe — Attacker · *"After you. No, truly — I insist."*
**Appearance:** Foppish pirate duelist, lace cuffs somehow always dry, feathered tricorne, pencil mustache, rapier held like a conductor's baton.
**Story:** Former first blade of a pirate crew who quit over "creative differences" (they wanted to rob people; he wanted an audience). Joined the guild for the applause. His theatrical timing is mechanical: he's the anchor of every turn. Recruit: Ch. 2.
**Kit (3 specials):**
- **Passive — Flourish:** +10% damage per ally who ATTACKED AN ENEMY before him this turn (max +40%). Guards, heals, and buffs don't count — on defensive turns his damage collapses, which makes "acts last" a real decision, not a free +40% (red-team fix).
- **Skill — Riposte Stance (1⚡):** counters the next attack that targets him; if nothing attacks him before his next turn, the ⚡ is refunded.
- **Art — Coup de Grâce (2⚡):** heavy thrust; bonus damage vs. debuffed enemies.
- **Burst — Curtain Call (3⚡):** five-thrust finale; always counts as full Flourish regardless of what allies did — the one attack that ignores a turtle turn.

### Brant — Breaker · *"Anchor's down. Nothing moves."*
**Appearance:** Barrel-chested first mate, rope coiled across his torso like a bandolier, gray mutton chops, swinging a ship's anchor on six feet of chain.
**Story:** Marlowe's former shipmate and the reason the crew survived Marlowe's theatrics. Followed him ashore out of habit and stayed out of loyalty. A man of eleven words a chapter. Recruit: Ch. 2 (with Marlowe).
**Kit (2 specials — simple, huge):**
- **Passive — Deadweight:** his break damage cannot be reduced below 50% by any resistance (softened from full immunity so break-resistant boss phases stay designable).
- **Skill — Anchor Drop (1⚡):** one colossal break hit. The single biggest per-hit break number in the game.
- **Burst — Keelhaul (3⚡):** drags the anchor THROUGH the boss: massive damage + massive break.

### Maren — Defender · *"The tide breaks on me. That's all it ever does."*
**Appearance:** Tall shield-maiden, teal scale cloak, kelp-braided blond hair, a tower shield shaped like a cresting wave with water flowing over it perpetually.
**Story:** Last keeper of a drowned lighthouse order that swore to stand between the sea and the shore. The order is gone; the oath transferred. She quietly considers Hollowing a "dry drowning" and takes it personally. Recruit: Ch. 5.
**Kit (3 specials):**
- **Passive — The Wall Remembers:** converts a portion of damage she blocks (guarding or shielded) into Energy — **capped at +1⚡ per turn** (uncapped, she paid the party to turtle; red-team fix).
- **Skill — Interpose (1⚡):** redirect all single-target attacks aimed at one chosen ally to herself this turn.
- **Art — Tidewall (2⚡):** party damage cut, 1 turn.
- **Burst — The Lighthouse Stands (3⚡):** taunt + huge self-shield; while it holds, allies take zero splash damage.

### Nix — Healer · *"Drink it. Yes, all of it. Yes, it tastes like that on purpose."*
**Appearance:** Scowling mermaid apothecary in a brass tank-chair on wheels, medicine bottles racked like artillery shells, pince-nez she doesn't need but thinks makes her look serious.
**Story:** A reef healer whose reef went hollow and crystalline — the first NPC voice to describe Hollowing from a survivor-adjacent view. Abrasive because grief is easier as irritation. Her single-target heals are the strongest in the game because she refuses to lose ONE more thing. Recruit: Ch. 3.
**Kit (2 specials — simple, decisive):**
- **Passive — Bedside Manner (Terrible):** her heals on allies below 30% HP are +50% stronger.
- **Skill — Bitter Draught (1⚡):** strong single-ally heal + cleanse all debuffs.
- **Burst — Panacea (3⚡):** full heal one ally + 1-turn debuff immunity.

### Tobin — Supporter · *"The water already knows what he'll do. I just ask it nicely."*
**Appearance:** A small boy in a scholar's coat four sizes too big, sleeves rolled six times, three orbs of seawater floating around his head like patient moons.
**Story:** A fishing-town foundling who reads intentions in still water. Ansel vouched for him when the guild called him too young; Tobin is the one who later reads the pattern of the failing seal-sites in Ch. 8 — the game's plot-turn rests on its smallest unit. Recruit: Ch. 1 (starter).
**Kit (3 specials):**
- **Passive — Still Water:** at the start of each turn, shows which unit the boss's next single-target attack will hit (once per turn, passively).
- **Skill — Dampen (1⚡):** enemy ATK down.
- **Art — Read the Currents (2⚡):** reveal the boss's full next action + party DEF up; any ally who Guards through the revealed attack gains +1⚡ (information converts into decisions, so the kit doesn't decay on refights).
- **Burst — The Tide Confides (3⚡):** major ATK + DEF down on the boss.

## 🌿 EARTH

### Garrick — Attacker · *"First swing's the honest one."*
**Appearance:** Gray-bearded beast hunter built like the trees he sleeps under, bear-pelt mantle, twin braids in the beard, a greataxe notched from decades of first swings.
**Story:** Hunted every apex predator in the frontier, then watched the Hollowing make his life's knowledge obsolete — hollowed beasts don't behave like beasts. His Ch. 4 mission, where his tracking fails for the first time, teaches the player that the Mirror's copies aren't animals. Knew Greywick's herdsman by name — and stops saying it after the party puts down the Lowing Man in Ch. 6, because now he knows what the reflection was wearing. Recruit: Ch. 4.
**Kit (2 specials — simple, brutal):**
- **Passive — First Blood:** +40% damage against enemies at full HP.
- **Skill — Skull Cleave (1⚡):** heavy single-target chop.
- **Burst — The Old Way (3⚡):** colossal single hit; instantly kills non-boss adds below 25% HP.

### Borin — Breaker · *"Everything's got a load-bearing something."*
**Appearance:** Mole-kin sapper, velvet-furred, star-nose, mining goggles pushed up, candle-stub helmet, gauntlets tipped with digging claws.
**Story:** Guild surveyor who mapped half of Vessia's underground before the guild realized his "hobby maps" were priceless. He's the first to notice the Seam's existence — his tunnels keep bending around something that "doesn't want to be dug toward" (Ch. 8 setup). Recruit: Ch. 5.
**Kit (3 specials):**
- **Passive — Sapper's Eye:** the boss's break bar is visible in precise numbers to the player (not just a gauge) while Borin is fielded.
- **Skill — Undermine (1⚡):** break damage + the boss's next fixed action is skipped. Subject to the global delay rule: once between each boss action, never chained.
- **Art — Tunnel Charge (2⚡):** AoE break.
- **Burst — Bring Down the Ceiling (3⚡):** heavy break; if it staggers, the stagger lasts +1 turn.

### Tarn — Defender · *"Mountains don't taunt. They're simply there."*
**Appearance:** Elderly tortoise-kin knight, moss growing on his shell-shield, ceremonial spear used mostly as a walking stick, half-lidded eyes that miss nothing.
**Story:** Retired guild founder-era knight who un-retired the day Greywick fell, to the visible relief of everyone who's read his file. He fought beside Ansel for thirty years; in Ch. 7 he's the one who insists the party be the ones to put Ansel down: "Debts are paid by hand." Recruit: Ch. 6.
**Kit (2 specials — simple, patient):**
- **Passive — Old Growth:** while Tarn is above half HP, the party takes -10% damage. Not a taunt. A fact.
- **Skill — Bedrock (1⚡):** party damage cut, 1 turn.
- **Burst — Mountain's Patience (3⚡):** huge party damage cut + attackers take thorns damage, 2 turns.

### Maud — Healer · *"Sit DOWN, you're bleeding on my herbs."*
**Appearance:** Tiny druid grandmother, shawl of woven ivy, a basket of poultices bigger than she is, and a hedgehog familiar named Bristle riding the basket.
**Story:** Village wise-woman from the settlements nearest the frontier — she's treated half the roster's childhood scrapes and remembers ALL of them, loudly. Refuses to call the Mirror anything but "that rude thing." Her herb garden's slow recovery mirrors Greywick's (same background-recovery system, small scale). Recruit: Ch. 3.
**Kit (3 specials):**
- **Passive — Poultice Pouch:** her heal-over-time effects stack with each other.
- **Skill — Poultice (1⚡):** single-ally heal-over-time, 3 turns.
- **Art — Herbal Round (2⚡):** party heal-over-time, 2 turns.
- **Burst — Grandmother's Garden (3⚡):** instant party heal + a field that ticks party healing for 3 turns.

### Fennel — Supporter · *"According to my charts, we are standing somewhere extremely defensible. You're welcome."*
**Appearance:** Lanky geomancer-cartographer, pith helmet, map tubes rattling on his back like quivers, a theodolite that unfolds into a staff.
**Story:** Royal surveyor who quit the court when they shelved his report on "anomalous subterranean acoustics" (the Seam, ten years early — nobody listened). Being right is his love language. He and Borin have a running feud about whose maps are canonical. Recruit: Ch. 5.
**Kit (3 specials):**
- **Passive — Surveyed Ground:** party DEF +5% at all times (he has, of course, already charted the battlefield).
- **Skill — Rampart Sketch (1⚡):** DEF up, one ally.
- **Art — Redoubt (2⚡):** DEF up, party.
- **Burst — Ley Bastion (3⚡):** raises charged terrain: Earth allies' damage up + party DEF up, 3 turns.

## ⚡ THUNDER

### Raiko — Attacker · *"..."* (his sprite bows; he has never spoken on-screen)
**Appearance:** Storm ninja, tattered indigo scarf longer than he is tall, twin kodachi worn reversed, lightning-scar tattoos that glow faintly when his Energy is full. The GS homage unit, worn proudly.
**Story:** Sole survivor of a mountain shrine that guarded "the sky's temper." He took a vow of silence when the shrine fell and communicates in bows, glances, and violence. Kaizan has challenged him to a duel eleven times; Raiko has not noticed. Recruit: Ch. 6.
**Kit (3 specials):**
- **Passive — Storm's Herald:** starts every battle with **1 of his 3 Energy bars** filled (not the full gauge — no turn-1 Heavenrender, and Garrick keeps the opener niche).
- **Skill — Flash Step (1⚡):** instant strike + his next basic this battle charges +2⚡.
- **Art — Twin Bolt (2⚡):** two heavy strikes (can split targets).
- **Burst — Heavenrender (3⚡):** vanish, then the screen whites out — massive multi-hit on one enemy.

### Volta — Breaker · *"HOLD STILL, I built this for EXACTLY this!"*
**Appearance:** Gremlin engineer girl, wild teal hair escaping welding goggles, grease-stained jumpsuit, piston gauntlets that hiss steam when she idles.
**Story:** Built her first piston gauntlet at nine to open pickle jars; the rest is escalation. Fascinated by hollowglass as a *material* — her Ch. 5 workshop scene ("it doesn't resonate, it ABSORBS, that's impossible") gives the player the first mechanical hint of what the Mirror is. Recruit: Ch. 3.
**Kit (3 specials):**
- **Passive — Counter-Torque:** double break damage vs. enemies that are charging or delayed (synergy: Borin's Undermine).
- **Skill — Piston Jab (1⚡):** quick break hit.
- **Art — Overclock (2⚡):** her break damage +50% for 2 turns (stacks with Counter-Torque; she vibrates visibly).
- **Burst — Pile Bunker Maximum (3⚡):** one apocalyptic piston strike: huge break + damage.

### Galvan — Defender · *"Strike ME. I conduct."*
**Appearance:** Paladin in coil-wound armor, copper wire wrapping every plate, a banner-spear whose pennant crackles with static. His hair will not stay down.
**Story:** A lightning-struck knight who woke up convinced the bolt was a calling (the church disagrees; the guild doesn't care). His theology is suspect but his physics are impeccable: he genuinely does conduct. Comic relief who turns dead serious the moment someone's in danger. Recruit: Ch. 4.
**Kit (3 specials):**
- **Passive — Lightning Rod:** stores a **Charge** each time he's hit (max 5).
- **Skill — Conduct (1⚡):** taunt 1 turn + counter every hit taken while taunting (counters generate Charge).
- **Art — Discharge (2⚡):** AoE thunder damage, consumes all Charges, scaling per Charge.
- **Burst — Storm Bastion (3⚡):** taunt ALL enemies + damage cut on himself + every hit taken generates double Charge, 2 turns.

### Sen — Healer · *"Hearts stop. That is not the same as ending."*
**Appearance:** Shaved-headed monk, storm-gray robes, palms wrapped in prayer-inscribed bandages, a heartbeat-slow walk. Faint static crackles when he heals.
**Story:** A mountain monk who once restarted his master's heart with a palm strike and has been asking the sky what that means ever since. He tends to the guild's dead and dying without flinching — which makes him the only unit who is *calm* during Ch. 7. The game's sole revive lives here, deliberately (Open Question §10.2). Recruit: Ch. 6.
**Kit (2 specials — simple, sacred):**
- **Passive — Last Breath:** once per battle, the first ally struck by lethal damage survives at 1 HP instead (reworked from a near-null DoT clause; the revive-adjacent unit gets the death-adjacent passive).
- **Skill — Palm of Stillness (1⚡):** modest single-ally heal + cleanse one debuff.
- **Burst — Restart the Heart (3⚡):** revive one fallen ally at 40% HP. The game's ONLY revive.

### Milla — Supporter · *"Package for— oh, it's URGENT urgent? Say no more."*
**Appearance:** Courier girl mid-stride even when standing still, winged boots, overstuffed satchel, a static-charged cloak that streams behind her like a lightning trail.
**Story:** The guild's contract courier who kept outrunning hollowed beasts on delivery routes until someone did the math on her survival odds and handed her a combat license. Knows every road, shortcut, and gossip chain in Vessia — the party's unofficial intelligence network. Recruit: Ch. 2.
**Kit (3 specials):**
- **Passive — Never Late:** the first time each battle an ally would be one bar short of a special, Milla grants +1⚡ automatically.
- **Skill — Hand-Off (1⚡):** give one ally +2⚡ (equal generation to her basic attack, but placed exactly where it's needed — logistics, not just tempo; red-team fix).
- **Art — Express Route (2⚡):** two chosen allies +2⚡ each (net-positive; her role-defining move, flagged for tuning).
- **Burst — Special Delivery (3⚡):** one ally to FULL Energy + ATK up.

## ✨ LIGHT

### Auren — Attacker · *"I was his squire. I ran. Never again."*
**Appearance:** Tarnished white plate that no longer shines no matter how it's polished, torn half-cape, a greatsword with a cracked sun-disc pommel. Handsome face, tired eyes.
**Story:** **Ansel's former squire.** He froze at a critical moment years ago; a village paid for it, and Ansel — who forgave him instantly — could never make Auren forgive himself. He joined the guild under a different name. Ch. 7 is his crucible: the player fights his mentor's reflection with him in the party, and his Burst gets unique dialogue. Recruit: Ch. 5.
**Kit (3 specials):**
- **Passive — Undimmed:** +15% ATK for each ally below half HP or fallen (max +60%). He is strongest when everything is going wrong.
- **Skill — Dawncut (1⚡):** clean holy strike.
- **Art — Oathstrike (2⚡):** heavy blow; +50% if any ally has fallen this battle.
- **Burst — REDEMPTION (3⚡):** a sunrise-sized cut. Damage scales with Undimmed. Unique line in Ch. 7.

### Solon — Breaker · *"Alone, a ray of light. Together, a lens."*
**Appearance:** Serene monk in white-gold robes with a prismatic hem, carrying an oversized glass PRISM-STAFF taller than he is, with a floating glass halo-ring above his head. (Staff + halo added so his silhouette never reads as "the other bald monk" next to Sen at sprite scale.)
**Story:** A contemplative who studied how light behaves inside crystal — which made him the world's accidental expert on hollowglass the moment the Hollowing began. He finds the Mirror *theologically fascinating*, which unnerves everyone. Provides the Ch. 8 exposition on why the Seam bends light wrong. Recruit: Ch. 7.
**Kit (3 specials):**
- **Passive — Refraction:** his break damage +15% per distinct ally who hit the boss this turn (the team-play breaker).
- **Skill — Prism Palm (1⚡):** break strike.
- **Art — Focusing Lens (2⚡):** this turn, ALL allies' attacks add small break damage.
- **Burst — Spectrum Break (3⚡):** break damage ×(distinct attackers this turn). With full team participation, the biggest break in the game.

### Lumen — Defender · *"⟡"* (it communicates in warm flickers)
**Appearance:** A pale-gold golem with a lighthouse-lantern head, cool white light spilling through armor seams, carrying a lamppost as a mace. Moths love it. It loves the moths. (Palette pushed cool/white to contrast Hearthgar's black-iron red.)
**Story:** An ancient temple guardian that wandered out of its ruined shrine following "the dimming" (its senses feel the Mirror as encroaching darkness). It adopted the guild — specifically, it decided the guild's weakest member at any moment is its lamplighter, and guards them absolutely. Nobody built it. Nobody knows who did. Arc-two bait. Recruit: Ch. 8.
**Kit (2 specials — simple, warm):**
- **Passive — Keeper of the Small Flame:** at the start of each turn, automatically shields the lowest-HP ally (small shield).
- **Skill — Lantern Ward (1⚡):** solid shield on one ally.
- **Burst — BEACON (3⚡):** big shields on the whole party + enemies' next attacks are drawn to Lumen.

### Celia — Healer · *"One more song. There's always one more song."*
**Appearance:** Traveling songstress, road-worn dress with festival ribbons sewn over the patches, an heirloom lyre, hair pinned with a tuning fork.
**Story:** She sang Ansel's ballads in every tavern in Vessia — the player hears one in Ch. 2, full and proud. After Ch. 7 the ballad returns as a slow lament, same melody, and her Burst quietly changes its animation's background music to it. She joined the guild to write true songs instead of pretty ones. Recruit: Ch. 4.
**Kit (2 specials — simple, resonant):**
- **Passive — Encore:** after Celia Bursts, all allies' next special this battle costs 1⚡ less.
- **Skill — Verse (1⚡):** party-wide small heal.
- **Burst — Standing Ovation (3⚡):** FULL party heal. The showstopper.

### Elior — Supporter · *"I've read about this. Hold on. I've read about *everything*, hold on—"*
**Appearance:** Ink-stained oracle-librarian, monocle, robes with page-corner bookmarks pinned like medals, four books orbiting him of their own accord.
**Story:** The royal archive's banished cataloguer — banished for filing the crown's official history under "fiction, optimistic." He joined the guild for access to field data. He is assembling a bestiary of the Hollowed that becomes the in-game codex UI: the player's encyclopedia is literally Elior's notebook. Recruit: Ch. 3.
**Kit (3 specials):**
- **Passive — Annotated Weakness:** party crit chance +10% against broken (staggered) enemies.
- **Skill — Cross-Reference (1⚡):** shred one enemy's elemental resistance.
- **Art — Underline (2⚡):** party crit rate up, 2 turns.
- **Burst — Grand Thesis (3⚡):** major resist shred + crit up + the enemy's stats become visible in full.

## 🌑 DARK

### Morrec — Attacker · *"It was my grandfather's sword. It ate him too."*
**Appearance:** Gaunt swordsman, prematurely white hair, black veins webbing up his sword arm, a longsword wrapped in chain that rattles when it's *hungry*.
**Story:** Heir to a cursed family blade that grants strength for blood and always collects. He joined the guild because dying usefully beats dying quietly, and he expects to do one of the two. Vesper is the only one who can slow the curse — their sidequest chain (Ch. 5–8) is the roster's darkest and ends with a choice. Recruit: Ch. 5.
**Kit (3 specials):**
- **Passive — The Price:** his specials cost HP as well as Energy — and deal the game's highest single-target raw damage. (Distinct from the Glasswright: Morrec pays to SPEND; the Glasswright pays to BUILD.)
- **Skill — Thirsting Cut (1⚡, small HP cost):** heavy strike.
- **Art — Feed the Blade (2⚡, moderate HP cost):** enormous strike; refunds the HP cost if it kills.
- **Burst — GORGE (3⚡, large HP cost):** the sword unwraps. Catastrophic single-target damage.

### Nyx — Breaker · *"Everything breaks. I just schedule it."*
**Appearance:** Hooded bounty hunter, chain-scythe coiled at the hip, a belt of folded wanted posters — several crossed out, one suspiciously blank.
**Story:** Vessia's most reliable monster-bounty hunter, in it strictly for coin — until Greywick, which she witnessed from a ridge and has never described to anyone. ## The Mirror Ruleset (Ch. 10 mirror match — how kits behave when copied)

The finale copies the player's own squad. Without rulings, a third of the roster breaks when mirrored. Global rules:

1. **Mirror copies have a Poise bar** (player-side break bar) — so the player's Breakers work against them. Mirrored BREAKER kits convert their break damage into bonus HP damage (copies attacking copies of a bar they don't have).
2. **"The boss" resolves to "the enemy with the highest max HP"** for any mirrored kit that references it (Marionne's self-hit, Vesper's drains, Nyx's scaling).
3. **Mirrored HP costs are paid, but copies cannot drop below 1 HP from their own costs** — mirror-Morrec burns himself to a sliver instead of suiciding. (Thematically correct: the reflection doesn't understand the price, only the shape of paying it.)
4. **Mirror-Sen keeps his revive.** Bringing your only revive to the final fight arms the enemy with one. That's not a bug; that's the fight. Budget encounter length for it.
5. **Information passives (Tobin's Still Water, Elior's reveals) are inert on the mirror's side** — the Mirror does not need to predict you. It already has.
6. **Mirror-Auren's Undimmed works fully:** his copy ramps as you kill its allies, creating a real kill-order decision. Protected feature — do not soften.
