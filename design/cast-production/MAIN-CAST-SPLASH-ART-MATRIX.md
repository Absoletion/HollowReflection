# Main Cast Splash-Art Production Matrix

## Shared rendering language

- Premium square anime-gacha key art with crisp tapered plum-black linework and cel-painted material rendering.
- Character anatomy follows the established detailed anime illustrations, not the compact battle-sprite proportions.
- Face, eyes, hands, signature equipment, and the primary action remain readable before ornamental detail.
- One dominant action line, one primary effect arc, and one supporting motif or environmental shape.
- Build the body around a clear C-curve or S-curve line of action. Use opposing shoulder and hip angles for contrapposto rather than symmetrical reference-sheet posture.
- Preserve deliberate negative space between limbs, torso, weapon, and major cloth shapes so the silhouette remains readable when filled solid black.
- Use controlled foreshortening on the signature weapon, casting hand, or nearest effect. Foreground scale must guide the eye back to the face rather than obscure it.
- Materials remain distinct: steel, blackened iron, leather, heavy cloth, glass, water, flame, and brass must not share one generic shine.
- Treat elemental magic as an actual scene light: sharp high-contrast reflections on metal, softer diffused color on skin and cloth, and visible intensity falloff with distance.
- Organize spectacle into three depth layers: a few soft foreground particles, the sharp primary midground ability effect, and restrained atmospheric background glow/smoke used to separate the silhouette.
- Element color is concentrated around the action. It does not tint every costume surface.
- Every luminous ability uses a small near-white hot core, a saturated elemental body, and desaturated atmospheric decay at the outer edge.
- Preserve the project’s neutral/material/energy hierarchy near 60/30/10. Complementary environmental hues may separate the unit from same-hue magic, but must not change the unit’s canonical palette.
- Safe square crop with the face in the upper-middle region and enough head/weapon margin for responsive Unit Library layouts.
- No title text, rarity text, checkerboard pattern, logo, watermark, UI frame, or mock transparency.
- Each image uses a character-specific atmospheric backdrop suitable for direct use as composed splash art.
- Matching icons are cropped from the approved splash master so face, hair, palette, and rendering remain identical.

## Icon crop contract

- Source only from the matching approved splash master.
- Square crop centered on face and upper shoulders, with hair silhouette intact.
- Eyes sit approximately 42% down from the icon’s top edge.
- Preserve one readable identity accessory when possible: Hale’s collar/scar, Cinnia’s wheat ornament/freckles, Katie’s glasses/cat clip, Tobin’s oversized collar, Hearthgar’s visor, Brigga’s soot/goggles, Marlowe’s hat/feather, Brant’s mutton chops, Milla’s scarf, Nix’s pince-nez.
- Avoid weapon heads, hands, text, high-intensity effect cores, and empty background dominating the crop.
- Runtime delivery: 512×512 WebP plus retained lossless PNG source.
